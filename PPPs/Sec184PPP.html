SECTION 184 - DETERMINATION AND REPORTING OF NOTIONAL LIFE (PPP)

184.01 GENERAL

This section relates to the calculation and reporting of pavement Notional Life of a given Notional Life 100 m Sub Section.

There are two applicable but separate technical rationale for the determination of the nominal life of discrete pavement segments. The two techniques are applicable to:

i. Pavements with asphalt thickness less than 50 mm,

ii. Pavements with asphalt thickness 50 mm or greater.

The application of this Standard Section requires use of the associated Spreadsheet Model.

184.02 DEFINITIONS

Deflection Measurement Sub Section - An individual sub section of deflection condition data in a Test Lane.

Notional Life - The notional service life of the pavement structure based upon the calculated number of years of trafficking to reach the allowable traffic loading.

Notional Life 100 m Sub Section - A section of pavement in a Test Lane made up of the minimum number of continuous Deflection Measurement Sub Sections to total 100 m or greater.

Project Asset - As defined in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements

Project Road - As defined in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements

Pavement Reporting Section - As defined in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements

Structural Life - Remaining structural life of pavement as measured by Notional Life

Spreadsheet Model - means the Notional Life Calculation Spreadsheet Model V1.0.xlsx

Test Lane - Each through traffic lane that Project Co must maintain within a Pavement Reporting Section, excluding auxiliary lanes less than 500 metres.

180.03 MEASUREMENT AND REPORTING OF PAVEMENT NOTIONAL LIFE

(a) Pavements with Asphalt Thickness Less than 50 mm

The Notional Life for this pavement type is determined from the distribution of lives after notional pavement reshaping to correct roughness and rutting but without notional strengthening, calculated as follows:

i. Testing shall be carried out during November and/or December.

ii. Measure the surface deflections in the left-hand wheel path of each lane using either a Falling Weight Deflectometer (FWD), Benkelman Beam, or Deflectograph. Deflection measurements are to be at intervals of 20 m or less. Pavement temperature shall be measured during the deflection testing in accordance with Austroads Guide to Asset Management - Technical Information - Part 15: Technical Supplements (2018).


iii. Convert the measured FWD, and Deflectograph maximum deflections to Benkelman Beam D0 values in accordance with Austroads (2011).

iv. For all deflection devices, the measured deflections are not to be adjusted for seasonal moisture variations.

v. Calculate the characteristic maximum deflection (CD) in the left-hand wheel path for each Notional Life 100 m Sub Section in accordance with Austroads (2011) and the Spreadsheet Model.

vi. Calculate the allowable traffic loading in Equivalent Standard Axles (ESAs) after notional pavement reshaping using the value of the CD in accordance with Austroads (2011) and the Spreadsheet Model.

vii. Calculate the Notional Life for each Notional Life 100 m Sub Section within the network in accordance with the Spreadsheet Model. An upper limit of 30 years is to be applied to the Notional Life. Notional Life shall be rounded down to the nearest whole year.

viii. Report results as described in Clause 184.03(c).

For this model, it is assumed that any pavement reshaping less than 50 mm does not provide any national strengthening.

(b) Pavements with Asphalt Thickness 50 mm or greater

The Notional Life for this pavement type is determined from the distribution of lives after resurfacing with a notional 50 mm thick, Size 14 mm Class 320 bitumen dense graded asphalt overlay, calculated as follows:

i. Testing shall be carried out during November and/or December.

ii. Measure the surface deflections in the left-hand wheel path of each lane using either a FWD, Benkelman Beam or Deflectograph. Deflection measurements are to at intervals of 20 m or less. Pavement temperature shall be measured during the deflection testing in accordance with Austroads Guide to Asset Management - Technical Information - Part 15: Technical Supplements (2018).

iii. Convert the measured FWD and Deflectograph maximum deflections to Benkelman Beam D0 values in accordance with Austroads (2011).

iv. For all deflection devices, the measured deflections are not to be adjusted for seasonal moisture variations.

v. Calculate the characteristic maximum curvature (CC) in the left-hand wheel path for each Notional Life 100 m Sub Section in accordance with Austroads (2011) and the Spreadsheet Model.

vi. Calculate the allowable traffic loading in ESAs after notional 50 mm overlay, using the value of the CC in accordance with Austroads (2011) and the Spreadsheet Model.

vii. Calculate the Notional Life for each Notional Life 100 m Sub Section within the network in accordance with the Spreadsheet Model. An upper limit of 30 years is to be applied to the Notional Life. Notional Life shall be rounded down to the nearest whole year.

viii. Report results as described in Clause 184.03(c)

(c) Reporting of Results

Notional Life is to be reported for each Notional Life 100 m Sub Section for every Test Lane, including all inputs and outputs from the Spreadsheet Model.
