SECTION 180 - RIDE QUALITY FOR NEW PAVEMENTS (PPP)

180.01 GENERAL

This section relates to ride quality performance for all new pavement construction of all finished roadways which form part of the Project Assets.

180.02 PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS

The finished surface of the pavement shall have a smooth longitudinal profile and comply with the minimum ride quality requirements specified. The upper limits for roughness at various locations are specified in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements.

180.03 DEFINITIONS

International Roughness Index (IRI qc) - The international measure of ride quality measured and recorded by modern Class 1 profile measuring devices in cumulative metres per kilometre in each wheel path and averaged together. For the purposes of this specification, roughness measurement is to be based on the IRIqc quarter car model.

Individual Lane Roughness Value - The mean value of the two wheel path IRIqc values measured on a 100 m sub‑section of a traffic lane.

Mean Lane Roughness Value - The mean of the Individual Lane Roughness Values within a Work Lot as determined by the appropriate test method.

Measuring Device - The device used for measurement of roughness must be:

(a) capable of measuring the longitudinal road surface profile along one or both wheel paths at least every 250 mm with a precision in elevation of 0.5 mm over a wave band of 1 to 30 metres;

(b) calibrated according to the relevant test methods.

Work Lot - Means any part of the work constructed or manufactured under essentially uniform conditions in a continuous operation and which is essentially homogeneous with respect to material properties, general appearance and construction or manufacturing process. Each lot must be no more than 2 km in length.

180.04 MEASUREMENT AND REPORTING OF RIDE QUALITY

(a) Scope of Testing

Unless otherwise approved by the State, the surface of all pavement construction must be tested for ride quality.

(b) Lot Testing

All work must be tested in Work Lots. Testing must include all traffic lanes constructed within each Work Lot including any freeway ramps but excluding shoulders and turn lanes. Project Co must provide 24 hours notice to the Independent Reviewer (where applicable) and the State when ride quality testing will be undertaken and the limits for each Work Lot.


(c) Measurement of Ride Quality

Project Co must measure the longitudinal road surface profile of each Work Lot in accordance with the relevant Test Method applicable to the particular test equipment being used as listed in VicRoads Standard Specification Section 175.

Unless otherwise approved by the State, ride quality measurement must be undertaken within three months after the application of the final surfacing layer.

(d) Reporting of Test Results

Results must be processed and reported for each 100 m sub‑section. Any sub‑sections less than 100 m must be included with the sub‑section immediately preceding it and the individual roughness calculated over the longer sub‑section. Project Co must provide the Independent Reviewer and the State with a copy of all test results representing the final roughness survey of the completed pavement works together with the calculated values of the individual lane roughness for each 100 m sub-section and mean lane roughness for each Work Lot.

180.05 PERFORMANCE STANDARDS

Project Co must comply with the requirements for both the Maximum Individual Lane Roughness and Maximum Mean Lane Roughness as follows.

(a) Individual Lane Roughness

Where an Individual Lane Roughness Value in any 100 m sub‑section is greater than the specified Maximum Individual Lane Roughness Value specified in Project Scope and Delivery Requirements, Project Co must rectify work within the sub‑section to comply with this requirement. The minimum length of rectification work undertaken must be 100 m.

All rectified 100 m sub‑sections must be re‑tested following completion of rectification work.

(b) Mean Lane Roughness

Where the Mean Lane Roughness Value of a Work Lot is greater than the Mean Lane Roughness specified in Project Scope and Delivery Requirements, Project Co must rectify the work within this Work Lot to comply with this requirement. The minimum length for any rectification work undertaken must be 100 m.

Project Co must re‑test the Work Lot following completion of any rectification work.

180.06 PAVEMENT RECTIFICATION

Project Co must bear the full cost of any necessary rectification work including the cost of any additional work required to the underlying or adjacent pavement to comply with this section.
