SECTION 653 ‑ BEARINGS

653.01 GENERAL

This section covers the requirements for the manufacture, testing, protective treatment, supply and installation of bearings.

Bearings shall be designed to support the loads and to accommodate the movements and rotations shown on the Drawings.

This section also applies to the manufacture, protective treatment and supply of any bearing attachment plate assemblies required to anchor the bearing to a substructure or superstructure component.

653.02 STANDARDS

The relevant standards are listed in AS 5100:2017 C12.

653.03 INFORMATION TO BE SUPPLIED

The Contractor shall submit the following information for each bearing type:

(a) name of manufacturer

(b) rated load and movement capacity

(c) dimensioned drawings showing all components.

HP At least 10 working days before the proposed commencement of manufacture the Contractor shall forward to the Superintendent, a certificate which details the information required above and which verifies compliance with this Specification.

653.04 DESIGN AND FABRICATION

Design of bearings shall comply with the requirements of AS 5100:2017.

653.05 TESTING OF BEARINGS

Testing of bearings shall be conducted in accordance with AS 5100:2017.

After testing, bearings shall be dismantled and examined for distress, warping, scoring or other effects which may affect the durability of the bearing.

A bearing will be rejected if it exhibits any signs of failure as described in the relevant clauses.

If a bearing is rejected, two additional bearings from the batch it represents shall be tested. If both bearings meet the requirements of this Specification, the remaining bearings in the batch will be accepted. Otherwise each remaining bearing in the batch shall be tested to determine its compliance with the Specification.

653.06 TEST CERTIFICATES

Prior to the installation of the bearings the Contractor shall submit to the Superintendent copies of certificates showing the test results of each bearing test. The test certificates shall show whether any tolerances have been exceeded or faults observed.


653.07 PROTECTIVE COATINGS

Steel components of bearings (other than stainless steel sliding surfaces) shall be coated with zinc‑in‑silicate in accordance with the requirements of Section 631 including preparation of surfaces.

All bolts, nuts and washers shall be hot- dip galvanized in accordance with Section 631.

Stainless steel sliding surfaces shall not receive protective coatings and care shall be taken to protect these surfaces from being damaged or coated during the application of the protective treatment to other surfaces.

653.08 SHOP ASSEMBLY

Bearings and ancillary components shall be shop-assembled at the manufacturer's works to ensure proper fitting of all parts.

653.09 MOVEMENT INDICATOR

If shown on the drawings, or specified, a movement indicator and suitably located scale shall be securely fixed to the bearings to enable shear movements to be monitored.

653.10 MARKING AND DELIVERY

All bearings shall be suitably marked to identify their location in the bridge. Bearings required to be preset for horizontal movement shall be clearly marked to ensure they are installed in the correct orientation.

Mating parts of bearings shall be permanently marked, and shall be supplied in sets held together with metal transit clips and/or bolts to prevent misalignment and/or damage of the components during transport and erection. Transit clips and/or bolts shall not be removed until final installation in the bridge or at the time specified on the drawings.

653.11 SITE STORAGE

Bearings stored on site prior to installation shall be protected from exposure to chemicals, fuel, oil, water, mud, dust, flood waters or any other deleterious substance.

If bearings are contaminated or damaged in any way, they shall be cleaned of all contaminants and offered to the Superintendent for inspection. Bearings that are damaged by contamination, inadequate storage or handling shall be replaced at the Contractor's expense.

653.12 TOLERANCES

Bearings shall be installed to the correct position and orientation as shown on the Drawings and, where applicable, in accordance with the bearing supplier's installation procedures. Bearings shall be installed level or to the specified grade ±0.5%.
