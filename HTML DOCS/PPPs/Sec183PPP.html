SECTION 183 - MEASUREMENT OF SKID RESISTANCE (PPP)

183.01 GENERAL

This section relates to Skid Resistance measurement for all existing finished roadways which form part of the Project Assets using a sideways-force coefficient measurement device.

183.02 DEFINITIONS

5 m Section - means a 5-metre length of pavement in a Test Lane, or a length greater than 5 metres where after taking into account all whole 5 m lengths of pavement in a Test Lane the remaining pavement length would otherwise be less than 5 metres.

Left SFC - Sideways Force Coefficient measured in the left wheelpath and reported in 5 m Section data.

Project Road - As defined in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements.

Processed SFC - The minimum of the four-point rolling average of 5 m Section SFC data within a Skid Resistance Section.

Processed Differential Friction Level (DFL) - The maximum difference between the four-point rolling average of Left SFC and adjacent four-point rolling average of Right SFC within a Skid Resistance Section.

Right SFC - Sideways Force Coefficient measured in the right wheelpath and reported in 5 m Section data

SFC - Sideways Force Coefficient; the measure of Skid Resistance, being the ratio of the sideways forces to the vertical reaction on the test wheel recorded as an individual measurement for a single sub-section of pavement surface 5 meters long.

Skid Resistance - A condition parameter that characterises the condition that a road surface alone makes to the total level of friction available at the contact patch between the road surface and vehicle tyre during acceleration, breaking, and manoeuvres.

Skid Resistance Sites - As defined in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements.

Skid Resistance Section - The extent of pavement included in a Skid Resistance Site.

Test Lane - As defined in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements.

183.03 MEASUREMENT AND REPORTING OF SKID RESISTANCE

(a) Scope of Testing

Unless otherwise approved by the State, the surface of all pavements within Skid Resistance Sections must be tested for Skid Resistance.

(b) Measurement of Skid Resistance

Project Co must measure the SFC condition of all pavements within Skid Resistance Sections in accordance with VicRoads sideways-force coefficient measurement device test method or suitable alternative as agreed by the State.

(d) Data Collection

The equipment used shall measure Skid Resistance using the sideways-force method over the appropriate range of operating speeds in both wheel paths.

Data sampling for skid resistance measurement must take place continuously and be stored at no more than 5 m intervals.

The equipment to be used must meet the minimum requirements in the manufacturer's instruction for the test device and in all applicable State and Austroads documents.

The equipment must be calibrated in accordance with the calibration procedure contained in the manufacturer's instruction for the test device and in accordance with all applicable State and Austroads documents.


(e) Skid Resistance Equipment Validation

Measurements made by the equipment shall be compared against historical data collected over a validation loop/site(s) specified by the State. The validation loop/site(s) shall cover a range of skid resistance. The minimum correlation coefficient for sideways force coefficient values measured by the equipment and historical data shall be greater than or equal to 0.7.

At any stage during the collection of data, the last successfully passed validation must be within the last 12 months.

Separate validations must be undertaken for each vehicle used to collect data.

(f) Calculation of SFC

SFC values shall be adjusted with all recognised correction factors as per the manufacturers instruction for the test device and in accordance with all applicable State and Austroads documents, and expressed as a positive, unsigned integer.

(g) Calculation of Processed SFC

Processed SFC shall be calculated by taking the minimum of the four-point rolling SFC average within each Skid Resistance Section. The Skid Resistance for each Skid Resistance Section shall be calculated in accordance with VicRoads sideways-force coefficient measurement device test method or suitable alternative as agreed by the State.

(h) Calculation of Processed DFL

Processed DFL shall be calculated by taking the maximum difference between the four-point rolling average of Left SFC and adjacent four-point rolling average of Right SFC within a Skid Resistance Section.

(i) Reporting of Test Results

All test results shall be reported in 5 m intervals and in accordance with VicRoads sideways-force coefficient measurement device test method or suitable alternative as agreed by the State.

  • Results shall also be reported as Processed SFC per Skid Resistance Section.

(j) Measurement Repeatability and Bias

The repeatability of skid resistance measurements must be assessed by undertaking a minimum of 5 runs over the validation loop/site(s) specified by the State as part of the validation process prior to each annual data collection and measurement. The minimum correlation coefficient for sideways force coefficient values measured at each loop/site shall be greater than or equal to 0.9.
