SECTION 181 - MEASUREMENT OF RIDE QUALITY FOR EXISTING PAVEMENTS (PPP)

181.01 GENERAL

This section relates to pavement ride quality measurement for all existing finished roadways which form part of the Project Assets.

181.02 DEFINITIONS

International Roughness Index (IRIqc) - The international measure of ride quality measured and recorded by modern Class 1 profile measuring devices in cumulative metres per kilometre in each wheel path and averaged together. For the purposes of this definition, roughness measurement is to be based on the IRIqc quarter car model.

Individual Lane Roughness Value - The mean value of the two wheel path IRIqc values measured on a 100 m sub‑section of a traffic lane.

Pavement Reporting Section - As defined in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements.

Test Lane - Each through traffic lane that Project Co must maintain within a Pavement Reporting Section, excluding auxiliary lanes less than 500 metres.

10 m Section - A 10 metre length of pavement in a Test Lane, or a length greater than 10 m where after taking into account all whole 10 m lengths of pavement in a Test Lane the remaining pavement length would otherwise be less than 10 metres.

100 m Section - A 100 metre length of pavement in a Test Lane, or a length greater than 100 m where after taking into account all whole 100 m lengths of pavement in a Test Lane the remaining pavement length would otherwise be less than 100 metres.

Feature - A length of pavement influenced by the presence of geometric or other features within the roadway such as rail crossings, tram tracks, bridge abutments or roundabouts as nominated in the Feature Inventory.

Feature Section - A 10 m Section of pavement of which any portion contains a Feature included in the Feature Inventory.

Feature Inventory - As defined in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements.

181.03 MEASUREMENT AND REPORTING OF RIDE QUALITY

(a) Scope of Testing

Unless otherwise approved by the State, the surface of all pavements must be tested for ride quality.

(b) Lot Testing

All work must be tested in Pavement Reporting Sections. Project Co must provide 24 hours notice to the Independent Reviewer (where applicable) and the State when ride quality testing will be undertaken for each Pavement Reporting Section.

(c) Measurement of Ride Quality

Project Co must measure the longitudinal road surface profile of each Test Lane in accordance with Austroads Test Method-Measurements-AG:AM/T001.

(d) Data Collection

The equipment used shall measure longitudinal road profile simultaneously in both wheel paths over the wavelength range of 0.5 metres to 50 metres over the appropriate range of operating speeds.

Data sampling for roughness measurement must take place at no more than 50 millimetre intervals. Height measurement shall be at an accuracy of ± 0.2 millimetres. Each wheel path profile shall be analysed by simulating the travel of a "quarter‑car" model to determine the roughness for each wheel path.

The inertial laser profilometer equipment to be used must meet the minimum requirements in Austroads Test Method AG AM/T001.

The profilometer must be calibrated in accordance with the calibration procedure contained in Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T001.

(e) Profilometer Equipment Validation

Equipment shall be validated in accordance with Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T001.

At any stage during the collection of data, the last successfully passed validation must be within the last 12 months.

Separate validations must be undertaken for each vehicle used to collect data.

In accordance with Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T001, Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T003 validation procedure must be adopted for the validation, and its check limits passed.

(f) Calculation of Roughness

Roughness shall be calculated as the result of analysis of the actual longitudinal road profile of each wheel path.

The roughness for each 100 m Section and Pavement Reporting Section shall be calculated in accordance with Clause 7 of Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T001. IRI results must be reported to two decimal places (m/km), and NRM shall be rounded to the nearest integer (counts/km).

(g) Reporting of Test Results

All reporting of test results shall be in accordance with Austroads Specification AG:AM/S001 except:

(i) survey data shall be marked as invalid if more than 20% of the data within a 10 m Section is collected:

  • when the road surface is wet; or
  • outside the speed constraints of the equipment.

The figure of 20% in clause (g)(i) above replaces the figure of 50% in Austroads Specification AG:AM/S001 under the heading Data Flag.

A 100 m Section shall be marked as containing invalid survey data if more than 20% of the 10 m Sections contained within a 100 m Section are invalid.

Results shall be processed and reported initially into 10 m Sections. The 10m Sections shall then be processed and reported in 100 m Sections.

The IRI values for each 100 m Section shall be the average IRI value of all 10 m Sections within the 100 m Section which do not contain invalid data and are not Feature Sections.

Project Co must provide the Independent Reviewer (where applicable) and the State with a copy of all test results representing the final roughness survey of each test, together with the calculated values of the mean lane roughness value for each Pavement Reporting Section, each 10 m Section, each 100 m Section, and all other information required by the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements.

Reporting unit for the left and right wheel path roughness shall be International Roughness Index (IRI) (m/km). The lane roughness shall be reported in IRI and NAASRA Roughness Meter (NRM) (counts/km).

Roughness results shall be reported for each 100 m Section, for both the left and right wheel paths, and recorded in the following formats:

(i) the average of left wheel path roughness in IRI;

(ii) the average of right wheel path roughness in IRI; and

(iii) the average of lane roughness in IRI and NRM.

When the Mean Value of Lane Roughness for a 100 m Section is greater than 7.6 IRI (200 NRM), lane roughness must be reported as 7.6 IRI (200 NRM).

The mean lane roughness value for each Pavement Reporting Section shall be the mean of the IRI calculations of the lane roughness for all 100 m Sections of all Test Lanes within a Pavement Reporting Section.

(h) Measurement Repeatability and Bias

Assessment of the repeatability of measurements and bias error must be undertaken in accordance with Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T004 as part of the validation process prior to each annual data collection and measurement.
