750.A01 GENERAL

Purpose

The purpose of this Section 750 PPP (Routine Maintenance) is to prescribe for road-based PPPs:

  • the minimum standards of performance required for the Routine Maintenance of road Pavements, Roadsides and structures, and

  • the minimum requirements for inspections, reporting and record-keeping and incident and emergency response.

750.A02 SCOPE OF WORKS

a) Project Co must inspect, maintain and report on each Pavement, Roadside and other Infrastructure Feature of the Project Roads to the specified Intervention Criteria to meet the specified functional requirements and other management responsibilities defined under legislation and other Reference Documents, including the Road Management Act and associated codes of practice. In doing so, Project Co must supply all materials, labour, plant and equipment to meet the requirements of the relevant VicRoads Standard Specifications.

b) Project Co must provide an emergency response resource and capability to assist with emergency incidents on Project Roads.

c) Routine Maintenance Strategy - Project Co must develop, maintain and implement a Routine Maintenance Strategy, incorporating inspections, programming and work activities to:

(i) Ensure public safety;

(ii) Meet the Intervention Criteria to minimise the rate of deterioration of the Infrastructure;

(iii) Sustain the functional requirements of each Infrastructure Feature;

(iv) Recognise and respond to the requirements of Road Users; and

(v) Address all requirements of Standard Section 750 PPP

d) Functional Requirements - The functional requirements for each Infrastructure Feature are prescribed in Table 750.A051.


750.A03 DEFINITIONS

a) Asset Management System or AMS - means the Asset Information Management System as defined in the Project Deed

b) Clear Zone - An area constructed and/or landscaped in the Road Reserve that is free of obstructions that enables vehicles that leave the sealed carriageway at the signposted speed to recover and safely return to the sealed carriageway.

c) Corrugations - the appearance of transverse ripples in the Pavement surface.

d) Critical Location - A location where the road alignment and/or Pavement width and/or geometry are not typical for the design speed of the road, and are subsequently identified by additional markings or furniture to guide the travelling public (cars, trucks, motorcycles, bicycles, and pedestrians).

e) Delamination - a failure mode of Pavements where there is a loss of an area of the Pavement surface (wearing course) exposing lower layers of the Pavement.

f) Emergency Response - as described in 750.F

g) Failed Pavement - Pavement that:

  • has experienced deformation greater than 25 mm in depth, measured with a 1.2 m straightedge transverse, or a 3 m straightedge longitudinal and where one or more of the following RM Defects exist:

(i) cracking greater than 50% of the area as described in RM114(c) "Crocodile Cracking";

(ii) cracking greater than 5 lineal metres per square metre of Pavement as defined in RM113 Crack Sealing;

(iii) potholing as defined in RM111 Pothole Patching;

(iv) rutting;

(v) shoving;

(vi) pumping of Pavement material;

(vii) Delamination; or

(viii) Corrugations

  • is unstable or visually moving under traffic; or

  • contains potholes that have been repaired more than twice.

h) Financial Close - as defined in the Project Deed

i) Flushed/Bleeding Surface - Where there is surplus binder on the road surface which is slippery in wet or frosty weather or becomes soft in hot weather.

j) Hazard - An event, condition or substance in the Maintenance Phase Area which has the potential to cause harm to the health and safety of persons.

k) Infrastructure Feature - a built or natural element within the Maintenance Phase Area and described in Table 750.A041.

l) Intervention Criteria - The condition level at which a RM Defect is recorded and either rectified or scheduled for rectification within a given Response Time pursuant to Table 750.H011

m) Litter - Any debris within the Maintenance Phase Area such as cardboard, paper, plastic, refuse, glass, metal, hypodermic needles, rubber, tyres, vehicle parts, building rubble, dead animals, excessive vegetative matter or anything of a like nature that was not installed during the construction of, or subsequent maintenance and renewal activities for, the Maintenance Phase Area.

n) Maintenance Phase Area - means either the Operational Phase Area or the Maintenance Phase Area (as applicable), as defined in the Project Deed.

o) Non-road Infrastructure - Any infrastructure in, on, under or over a road which is not defined as Road Infrastructure, such as utilities.

p) Other Paved Areas - Any paved areas other than the through carriageways and may include footpaths, shared use paths, medians and traffic islands. Paving may include, but not be limited to concrete, brick paving or asphalt surfacing.

q) Pavement - Any area of the road formation that includes a sealed surface, unsealed surface and shoulder.

r) Project Co - as defined in the Project Deed.

s) Response Code - A code, designated by a letter from 'A' to 'E', that indicates the control mechanism and Response Time for responding to a particular Hazard on a particular road.

t) Response Time

(i) For Hazards the maximum time to respond to a Hazard based on consideration of the Response Code, Road Maintenance Category and Response as detailed in Table 750.E023.

(ii) For RM Defects - The maximum time to respond to an RM Defect based on consideration of the Road Maintenance Category (abbreviated as RMC), Intervention Criteria and Response as detailed in Table 750.H011

u) Road Infrastructure - The physical components of a Roadway, pathway or shoulder, including built structures, traffic control and advisory signs, materials, and drainage systems.

v) Road Maintenance Category - A nominated category for each road determined according to an assessment of risk, taking into account factors such as road classification, road type and volume and type of traffic. Designated by a number from '1' to '6' and published in the VicRoads Register of Public Roads and the VicRoads Road Management Plan.

w) Roadside - Any area within the Road Reserve other than the Pavement, such as the median or grassland.

x) Road Reserve - All of the land that is within the Maintenance Phase Area.

y) Roadway - The area of the road that is used for the driving or riding of vehicles (including bicycles) but does not include driveways or other private access roads from adjoining land.

z) Road Users - People who travel on the road, which includes cyclists, drivers of vehicles, passengers in vehicles, pedestrians and road workers.

aa) Routine Maintenance - A regular program of activities including, but not limited to, inspections, repairs and other works and associated reports designed to address the requirements of Clause 750.A02(c).

bb) RM Defect - is a deficiency within any of the Infrastructure Features identified in Table 750.A041 that has reached or exceeded the Intervention Criteria detailed in Table 750.H011.

cc) Rural Area - as defined in the Project Deed

dd) Safe Intersection Sight Distance (SISD) - As defined in the Austroads Guide to Road Design

ee) Safety Sign - A road sign that is a Regulatory sign or a sign that provides the driver with advice about the safe use of a road.

ff) Service and Tourist Signs - as defined in AS1742

gg) Standard Job - A description of an activity to restore and/or repair a component of an Infrastructure Feature element to a defect-free condition. A list of Standard Jobs is detailed in Table 750.A041.

hh) Structure - means a Structural Asset as defined in the Project Deed.

ii) State - as defined in the Project Deed.

jj) TGSI - Tactile Ground Surface Indication as specified in AS1428.4

kk) Traffic Management Centre - The VicRoads Traffic Management Centre that monitors and coordinates the operation of the arterial road and freeway network.

ll) Unit of Work - Base unit of measurement of a standard job.

mm) Urban Area - as defined in the Project Deed.

750.A08 TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT REQUIREMENTS FOR ROUTINE MAINTENANCE WORKS

All works must be undertaken in accordance with the Code of Practice for Worksite Safety - Traffic Management (RMA 2004), and must be defined as either:

1. On-Road / Pavement works - must be assessed as Mobile Works, Very Short-Term Works, Short Term Works or Long Term Works, whichever is the most appropriate.

2. Off-Road / Roadside works (including shoulder grading and grass mowing) that are undertaken from the pavement - must be assessed as Very Short-Term Works - Frequently Changing Works Area. The requirements of Figure 3 of the Code of Practice must be achieved for sight distance and adequate passing width for two-way traffic.

Off-Road / Roadside works that are undertaken from the Roadside can be undertaken as Very Short Term Works. Activities that may need some encroachment into the Roadway that can be undertaken safely during gaps in traffic (e.g. a grass mower manoeuvring around an obstacle whilst mowing the Roadside or median) must be subject to a risk assessment. The minimum roadworks signing must be in accordance with Figure 3 of the Code of Practice.


PART 750.B - PAVEMENT MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS

750.B01 RM100 SEALED SURFACE

(a) General

Maintenance of sealed pavement must be carried out in accordance with the RM100 series of Standard Jobs and RM100 in Table 750.H011.

The use of cold mix asphalt must be restricted to emergency works. Patching carried out using cold mix asphalt must be replaced within 48 hours with hot mix asphalt. Project Co shall ensure that all cold mix patches are entered into the AMS, showing the treatment date for replacement based on Pavement RM Defect. The AMS shall identify location, required treatment date and actual treatment date.

Treatments must ensure that the finished surface matches the texture of the surface and shape of the pavement or shoulder. The surface level of any treatment must not vary from the existing surface level by more than 10 mm under a 3 metre straight edge. Pre-existing line marking, road markings and raised reflective pavement markings must be immediately reinstated after any repair.

Pavement joints created by maintenance treatments in bituminous and concrete surfaced pavements and shoulders must be sealed immediately to prevent the ingress of water.

Loose aggregate resulting from sealing of patching, regulation, crack sealing or surface treatments must be removed from the road pavement surface within 48 hours of completion of the seal, crack sealing or surface treatment. Project Co must maintain appropriate signage to warn motorists of the loose aggregate until it has been removed.

Any residual grit on the Roadway surface from spills or other remedial works must be removed prior to any speed restrictions being removed and the road deemed to be in normal operational use.

(b) Specific Requirements

RM131 Digouts

Failed Pavement areas must be excavated and replaced with the appropriate treatment.

The failed area must be squared up to produce a regular shaped patch.

All digouts must conform to the following performance criteria for surface materials, texture, line and level for a period of 6 months from the time of sealing:

(i) no point on the sealed surface of each digout must lie more than 10 mm below a 3 metre straight edge laid in any direction; and

(ii) the treated area must be free of cracking, and not be Flushed / Bleeding and / or possess stripping.

(c) Pavement cleaning

RM116 Pavement Cleaning

Further to the requirements for RM116 in Table 750.H011, Project Co is required to clean (sweep) all visible accumulated debris, granular material or obstacles from Pavements within the Maintenance Phase Area at a frequency of not less than once every six months.


750.B02 RM200 and RM300 SHOULDERS AND UNSEALED SURFACES

(a) General

Maintenance of shoulders and unsealed pavement must be carried out in accordance with RM221 and RM311 Standard Jobs in Table 750.H011. Where the shoulder is sealed in part width, the full width of the formed shoulder must be maintained.

The maintenance of unsealed pavements and shoulders must include:

(i) regular attention to Corrugations, potholes, depressions and scours;

(ii) maintenance of pavement and shoulder cross slopes; and

(iii) attention to drainage to allow water to flow freely across the pavement and shoulder.

Project Co is required to supply and place any additional material for the repair of unsealed shoulders and pavement.

(b) RM221 Unsealed Shoulder

Project Co must maintain all unsealed shoulders to at least the crossfalls and widths existing at Financial Close.

750.B03 RM400 DRAINAGE

(a) General

Maintenance of drainage systems must be carried out in accordance with the RM400 series of Standard Jobs and Table 750.H011.

Project Co must ensure that all drainage systems on the Road Reserve function to prevent:

(i) scouring of gravel or earth pavements, shoulders and drains;

(ii) softening of subgrade or soil formation causing failure or distortion of the pavement;

(iii) the pavement and subgrade becoming unstable due to excessive moisture and causing damage to the bitumen surface;

(iv) extensive land slips on both cuttings and embankments; and

(v) flood and scour damage to adjacent structures, buildings and land.

(b) RM411 Surface Drains and Verges

Drainage systems must be maintained to allow the free flow of water from the pavement, across the shoulder and verge and into and along any longitudinal drain lines. Any build-up of material on the Road Reserve that restricts the free flow of water must be removed.

Project Co must:

(i) keep all table, catch and diversion drains clear and operational;

(ii) keep all existing scour protection work operational;

(iii) redirect concentrations of water from passing directly over shoulders and batters of cuttings and embankments;

(iv) sustain the growth of grass and other suitable vegetation on batters, the adjoining Roadside and in drains on steep grades where scour is taking place;

(v) minimise disturbance of existing surface in areas prone to scour and use wide flat based drains;

(vi) ensure that all surface excavations are left so they will not scour; and

(vii) maintain any chute drains.

(c) RM412 Sub-surface Drains

Further to requirements for RM412 Sub-surface Drains in Table 750.H011, flush and outlet points are usually marked by either one or more ceramic markers or a permanent stake. These markers must be replaced as required. The outlet aprons and vermin guards of sub-surface drains must be kept clean of weeds and the vermin guards securely in place and effective at all times.


(d) RM413 Culverts, Pit Cleaning and Stormwater Drains

Further to the requirements of RM413 in Table 750.H011, Project Co must clean out all pits and the culverts or pipes cleared of any obstructions.

(e) RM414 Culvert and Pit Repair

Further to the requirements of RM414, Project Co must ensure that all repairs are carried out in accordance with VicRoads drawings and Standard Specifications

PART 750.C - ROADSIDE MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS

750.C01 GENERAL

Project Co must provide an aesthetically pleasing Roadside of clean and tidy appearance, consistently free of visible weeds, litter, graffiti and other RM Defects required to be addressed through the maintenance activities described in RM400, RM500, RM600 and RM800 series of Standard Jobs.

(a) General

All works carried out on the Roadside must be undertaken to prevent or minimise any disturbance to native or other planted vegetation or native fauna. Project Co must comply with:

(i) Victorian State Government planning provisions relating to native vegetation retention, environmental protection and conservation;

(ii) maintenance requirements contained within the VicRoads Roadside Handbook - Environmental Guidelines for Road Construction and Maintenance Workers; and

(iii) protection of landmarks and points of historic/cultural interest.

Regenerating native vegetation, including native grasses and other ground cover species must not be disturbed. Project Co is responsible for:

  • the reinstatement of any Roadside areas that have been damaged as a result of any maintenance activities, including herbicide spraying; and

  • maintaining a tidy appearance for the Roadside

750.C02 RM500 CONTROL OF ROADSIDE VEGETATION

Project Co must undertake Roadside maintenance activities on Roadside areas, including vegetation in accordance with the RM 500 series of Standard Jobs in Table 750.H011.

(a) RM511 Grass Mowing and RM512 Edge Trimming

Further to the requirements in Table 750.H011 Project Co must ensure that grass cuttings from areas subject to mowing or edge trimming are not left on pavements, shoulders, kerb and channel or in drains and that there is no uncut grass between passes or around or under trees or road furniture. Project Co is to ensure there is no excessive clumping of grass or windrowing following grass cutting.

Grass mowing and edge trimming to 3 metres behind the line of guideposts must include the mowing and trimming of any grass on the shoulder and the removal of grass from the sealed pavement. Where there are no guideposts mowing must be to a width of 5 metres from the edge of pavement, where practicable.

For all other Roadside areas a tidy appearance must be maintained, including achieving the requirements in Table.H011.

(b) RM513 Grass and Weed Control

Herbicide may be used to provide a vegetation free surface behind kerb or back of sealed shoulder, around fittings, furniture, culverts, bridge abutments, beaching and along fence lines, joints between pavement and kerb and channel and other areas in the Roadside.

Unless specified otherwise, spraying must not exceed 200 mm from the back of kerb or edge of shoulder and 450 mm surrounding road furniture, guard fence or fencing.


(c) RM515 Tree and Shrub Management

(i) General

Project Co must undertake maintenance activities within the Roadside areas to ensure safety of all users and remove Hazards through inspection, including providing appropriate reporting to the State.

Fallen and damaged timber debris must be removed from the Roadside by Project Co. Debris is required to be removed immediately where it poses a Hazard to Road Users. Project Co must maintain the Roadside in a tidy appearance.

Project Co must instruct unauthorised persons cutting down trees or removing timber to stop. The person's name or the registration number of an accompanying vehicle must be obtained, and the occurrence immediately notified to the State.

Where trees from private property overhang the Road Reserve to such an extent that they affect Road Users, obscure signs or interfere with maintenance work, Project Co must carry out pruning work.

Where trees overhang from the Road Reserve into private property and a request from the land owner is made to remove them, Project Co must undertake any required works.

Project Co must replace all trees or shrubs which die or are damaged as a result of poor herbicide application or poor maintenance techniques including mowing or brush cutting. Replacement plants must be supplied by Project Co and must be in 15 cm pots and a minimum of height of 300 mm.

(iii) Tree and Shrub Plantations

Further to the requirements of RM515, Tree and Shrub Management in Table 750.H011, Project Co must maintain tree and shrub plantations including:

  • maintaining ground cover under single trees or shrubs and in un-mulched plantations to the maximum height specified by cutting or providing a vegetation free surface;

  • maintain mulched or matted tree and shrub plantations in a weed free state;

  • removal of Litter; and

  • unless otherwise specified remove existing tree guards when the plant height exceeds 1.5 times the height of the tree guard.

(d) Noxious Weeds:

Project Co must make itself familiar with the declared noxious weeds proclaimed under the Catchment and Land Protection Act 1994 and be able to recognise them when they appear on the areas to be maintained.

Project Co must control all noxious weeds, and other pest plants, throughout the Road Reserve by spraying with a herbicide and/or removing and disposing off-site. All spraying and removal must be carried out so as to avoid damage to surrounding vegetation and minimal disturbance to soil surface.

Project Co must comply with the requirements of any documented weed or pest plant management plan applicable to the Maintenance Phase Area and must co-operate with any other agency or contractor involved in the management of weeds or pest plants on the Road Reserve or adjacent land.

750.C03 USE OF HERBICIDES

All personnel involved in the storage, handling and application of herbicides must hold appropriate licenses for such works, including but not limited to those required by the Public Health and Wellbeing Act 2008. Project Co must handle and apply herbicides in accordance with the manufacturer's recommendations and ensure that all personnel handling and applying herbicides comply with relevant legislation regulating the use of herbicides.

Project Co must not spray herbicides in any areas where the adjacent land use includes organically farmed products or other such sensitive receptors.

Project Co must keep records of all herbicide spraying activities including the operator's name, mixtures, materials, application rates, location, time and weather conditions.

750.C04 RM822 LITTER CONTROL

Project Co must collect and dispose of all Litter within the Road Reserve, including Litter in tree and shrub beds, according to the Intervention Criteria. Project Co must dispose of the Litter at a registered tip site.

750.C05 RM820 REST AREAS

Project Co must inspect, clean and maintain rest areas, wayside stops and truck stops as specified in Table 750.H011.

Where Litter bins and toilets are provided at these sites they must be kept in a clean and tidy condition. Project Co must arrange to empty Litter bins as specified and dispose of the contents at a registered tip site.

The pavement area of all rest areas, truck stops, formal bus stops within the Road Reserve, vehicle parks and less official stopping locations adjacent to the road pavement must be maintained by Project Co to the required Intervention Criteria for the various activities, including the RM100 (Sealed Pavement) series, RM311 (Unsealed Road) and RM221 (Unsealed Shoulder).

At rest areas where the limits of the rest area are not clearly defined, RM820(a) Litter Collection and RM820(c) Maintaining Grass Areas must be maintained to the specified Intervention Criteria for a minimum distance of 20 metres from all paved areas and facilities, or as far as can be practically maintained.

PART 750.F - EMERGENCY RESPONSE

750.F01 GENERAL

Under the Emergency Management Act 2013, VicRoads has statutory responsibility to meet emergency management obligations as:

  • the State Road Authority for Victoria; and

  • a Support agency or the lead agency for Transport, Engineering and Services Support (TESS) under Victoria's State Emergency Response Plan (SERP).

The roles in routine emergency incidents on roads over which VicRoads exercises control include:

(a) providing manpower, equipment and materials to clear and/or make roads safe;

(b) providing appropriate signing and traffic control assistance;

(c) selecting and establishing appropriate alternative detour routes; and

(d) providing information to the public on road and traffic conditions.

When required under the provisions of the SERP, VicRoads provides assistance to control agencies which includes:

(i) fulfilling the same roles as for emergency incidents;

(ii) assisting with the management of all road links, which can include emergency traffic management, escorting, route conditions and advice; and

(iii) providing support advice on transport and engineering matters which can include facilitating special transport needs and engineering advice such as the safety of bridges and land slips.

750.F02 PROJECT CO REQUIREMENTS

(a) Project Co is required to provide a 24-hour response capability to ensure safety of the public as a result of emergency incidents, including flooding, fires, storms and traffic crashes.

(b) Project Co must comply with a VicRoads' request for emergency response and make available any resources used in conjunction with the Deed.

(c) Control agencies for emergency response operations are designated in the Emergency Management Manual Victoria. Notwithstanding the provisions of the Project Deed, Project Co must provide assistance to and follow the directions of any control agency.

(d) If a section of road is affected by an emergency incident, Project Co must provide appropriate assistance to members of the public where possible.

The State will provide Project Co with one copy of relevant VicRoads Emergency Management Plan documentation for use during the Term.

Prior to commencement of work on-site, Project Co must provide the State with details of a single contact to be contactable 24 hours per day, and other contact numbers which will be used by Project Co during any emergency response.

750.F03 ACTION TO BE TAKEN BY PROJECT CO

(a) Initial Response

Project Co's initial emergency response must make the incident site safe for the public or otherwise assist the control agency. This may involve the setting up and operation of detour routes for the duration of the incident.


Any response which exceeds the capability of the Project Co is to be immediately notified to the VicRoads Traffic Management Centre.

Project Co must co-operate with the control agency and other emergency services.

Project Co is not responsible for salvaging vehicles which have been damaged in crashes and is not required to assist the owners of such vehicles except where necessary for the safety of the public or to reduce delays to traffic.

(b) Notification to VicRoads Traffic Management Centre

750.G03 SPILLAGES

Project Co must ensure that the relevant fire fighting authority is notified immediately of hazardous materials spillages.

Where spillage of chemicals or fuel is involved action must be taken under direction from the control agency or the Environment Protection Authority to prevent spillage from entering watercourses. Project Co must identify materials spilled by the HAZCHEM symbols and ensure that Project Co's personnel are suitably protected when handling hazardous material.

Road surfaces affected by spillages must be treated to provide safe driving conditions.

750.G04 FIRE PREVENTION

Project Co must observe Country Fire Authority and the Metropolitan Fire Brigade Roadside Management Guidelines. Fire prevention measures, including removal of flammable material from around bridge abutments, culverts, sheds, and other relevant structures must be done prior to the summer period. All flammable material within 9 metres of open fireplaces on the Project Roads must be cleared away.

Grass mowing must not be undertaken on days of Total Fire Ban. Other plant and equipment must not be used on days of Total Fire Ban. In all cases grass slashers, mowers, rotary hoes, burners on bitumen heaters and other plant and equipment must not be used contrary to any regulations, or by‑laws.

750.G05 HAZARDS TO ROAD USERS

Project Co must take the following steps to safeguard Road Users:

(a) provide any resources, including signs and traffic controllers necessary to protect Road Users;

(b) if the person responsible can be located, ask them to remove any obstruction immediately; and

(c) in cases of serious Hazards which may remain overnight, Project Co must manage any such incidents to ensure the safety of the travelling public.

750.G06 DAMAGE TO ROAD OR NON-ROAD INFRASTRUCTURE

Project Co must carry out works in such a manner as to not damage any Road Infrastructure belonging to another road authority or any Non-Road Infrastructure located within the Road Reserve. Any damage is to be notified to the infrastructure owner and repaired at Project Co's expense.

750.G07 ROADS WITH RAIL CROSSINGS AND TRAM LINES

The Accredited Rail Operator operating the railway or tram facility is responsible for maintaining the road pavement within 2.1 metres each side of the outer rails for rail crossings and 0.5 metres each side of outer rails for tram lines.

750.G08 WATER FROM MAINS, CHANNELS, STREAMS, DRAINS AND/OR DAMS

When it is necessary to obtain water from mains, channels, streams drains or dams, Project Co must obtain permission from the responsible water authority and observe conditions laid down.


750.G09 MANAGEMENT OF SPOIL AND EXCESS ROAD MAKING MATERIAL

Drainage spoil, unwanted shoulder material, digout material and other non-recyclable roadmaking material is not to be deposited on the Roadside. Bituminous material is to be taken off-site.

750.G10 STOCK AND OTHER ANIMALS

(a) Unattended Stock

Project Co must ensure that unattended stock on the Road Reserve do not represent a Hazard to Road Users.

Project Co must remove or arrange the removal of the unattended stock from the Road Reserve.

(b) Dead or Injured Animals

Project Co must remove and dispose (as appropriate) of dead animals from the pavement in accordance with Table 750.E022 and elsewhere within the Road Reserve within 24 Hours

Where an identification tag or marking enables the owner of the animal to be identified, Project Co must make reasonable attempts to notify the owner (or if the owner is not available, the Council) of the incident and the location of the animal. All identification tags or markings and details of actions taken must be recorded.

After removing any identification tags or markings, and with consideration of the owners requests, Council's procedures, Project Co may dispose of small dead animals by burying within the Road Reserve or disposal off-site and arrange for larger dead animals to be disposed of off-site at a knackery or licenced landfill. Any disposal must comply with all Laws.

Injured animals must be appropriately dealt with by Project Co, with consideration of the owners requests, assistance of veterinarians, wildlife carers, Council's procedures, etc. If there are injured animals on the Road Reserve which should be destroyed, the matter must be reported immediately to Victoria Police and Project Co must dispose of any animals so destroyed.

(c) Pest Animals

Project Co must note through routine road inspections areas of Roadside observed as harbour for pest animals such as rabbits and foxes.

Project Co must ensure that maintenance activities such as stockpiling of drainage material or other spoil do not become suitable sites for the establishment of pest animals.

750.G11 ABANDONED VEHICLES

Any vehicle that is left on an arterial road or freeway for a period greater than 48 hours is deemed to be abandoned.

Project Co may shift a vehicle which has been abandoned, has deemed to be abandoned or which Project Co has reasonable cause to believe has been abandoned and is a Hazard to Road Users. Project Co must take care not to damage vehicles when moving them and arrange for Victoria Police to access locked vehicles.

For any vehicle which Project Co suspects has been abandoned, the time, date, location and registration details must be recorded. If the Police advise that the vehicle is stolen, Project Co must have no further involvement. If the vehicle is not stolen, Project Co must affix an "abandoned vehicle sticker" to the vehicle 48 hours after the time of initial sighting and report these incidents to VicRoads Traffic Management Centre.

VicRoads Traffic Management Centre may request Project Co to assist with the management of abandoned vehicles. These requests must be deemed to be from the State.

Burnt out, stripped or remnants of vehicles abandoned on Road Reserves are to be regarded as scrap and must be removed by Project Co.


750.G12 PRIVATE ENTRANCES

Any person wishing to construct a private entrance to a property must first obtain the consent of the State. Any new entrance must be reported to the State.

The maintenance of the surfacing of a private entrance is the responsibility of the owner. Project Co must maintain all private entrance culverts.

750.G13 RESTORATION OF ROAD OPENINGS

Where road openings have been done by others and reinstatement has not satisfactorily completed, Project Co must undertake any temporary repairs and final restoration in accordance with Table 750.E022 and Table 750.H011.
