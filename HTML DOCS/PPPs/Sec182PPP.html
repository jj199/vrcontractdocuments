SECTION 182 - MEASUREMENT OF RUTTING FOR PAVEMENTS (PPP)

182.01 GENERAL

This section relates to pavement rutting measurement for all existing finished roadways which form part of the Project Assets.

182.02 DEFINITIONS

Pavement Reporting Section - As defined in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements.

Profilometer - Inertial laser profilometer.

Project Road - As defined in the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements.

Test Lane - Each through traffic lane that Project Co must maintain within a Pavement Reporting Section, excluding auxiliary lanes less than 500 metres.

Rut Depth - The vertical pavement displacement, measured from a stringline taken between lane edges (lane rut depth) or across a wheel path (wheel path rut depth).

100 m Section - A 100 metre length of pavement in a Test Lane, or a length greater than 100 m where after taking into account all whole 100 m lengths of pavement in a Test Lane the remaining pavement length would otherwise be less than 100 metres.

182.03 MEASUREMENT AND REPORTING OF RUTTING

(a) Scope of Testing

Unless otherwise approved by the State, the surface of all pavements must be tested for rutting.

(b) Lot Testing

All work must be tested in Pavement Reporting Sections. Project Co must provide 24 hours notice to the Independent Reviewer (where applicable) and the State when rutting testing will be undertaken for each Pavement Reporting Section.

(c) Measurement of Rutting

Project Co must measure the transverse road surface profile of each Test Lane in accordance with Austroads Test Method-Measurements- AG:AM/T009.

(d) Data Collection

Data sampling for rutting measurement must take place at no more than 100 millimetre intervals. Height measurement shall be at an accuracy of ± 0.5 millimetres.

The maximum wheel path rutting and lane rutting of each sampled profile shall be calculated by simulating the movement of a 1.2 metre and 3.0 metre straight edge respectively.

Where the variation between the two outer-most lasers, on any one side of the transverse profile, exceeds 60 millimetres, it shall be assumed that an undesirable edge condition has been encountered and the profile shall exclude the outer most point.

The inertial laser profilometer equipment to be used must meet the minimum requirements in Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T009.

The profilometer must be calibrated in accordance with the calibration procedure contained in Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T009.


(e) Profilometer Equipment Validation

Equipment shall be validated in accordance with Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T010.

At any stage during the collection of data, the last successfully passed validation must be within the last 12 months.

Separate validations must be undertaken for each vehicle used to collect data.

In accordance with Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T009, Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T010 validation procedure must be adopted for the validation, and its check limits passed.

(f) Calculation of Rutting

Rutting shall be calculated as the result of analysis of the actual transverse road profile of each wheel path and lane.

Lane rutting shall be calculated from the full transverse profile using the 3.0 m straight edge for each 100 m Section.

Wheel path rutting shall be calculated from the left and right profiles on the respective side of the centreline including the centreline point by simulating the maximum rut depth under a 1.2 m straight edge for each 100 m Section.

Reference shall be made to Austroads Guide to Asset Management Part 5 C: Rutting for guidelines for deriving results based on collected transverse road profile.

(g) Reporting of Test Results

Results must be processed and reported for each 100 m Section. Any sub‑sections less than 100 m must be included with the sub‑section immediately preceding it and the individual rutting calculated over the longer sub‑section.

Project Co must provide the Independent Reviewer (where applicable) and the State with a copy of all test results representing the final rutting survey of each test, together with the calculated values of the mean lane rutting value for each Pavement Reporting Section, each 100 m Section and all other information required by the Project Scope and Delivery Requirements.

Rutting results shall be reported in 100 metre lengths for the lane surveyed, for both the left and right wheel paths, and recorded in the following formats:

(i) the average of left wheel path rutting in mm

(ii) the average of right wheel path rutting in mm

(iii) the average of lane rutting in mm.

The average lane rutting in mm for each Pavement Reporting Section shall be the mean of the lane rutting for all 100 m Sections of all Test Lanes within a Pavement Reporting Section.

(h) Measurement Repeatability and Bias

Assessment of the repeatability of measurements and bias error must be undertaken in accordance with Austroads Test Method AG:AM/T012 as part of the validation process prior to each annual data collection and measurement.
