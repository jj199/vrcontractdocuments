750.A01 GENERAL

This specification details the standards of performance required for routine maintenance of road pavements, roadsides and structures. It also details inspection, emergency response and record requirements.

Unless otherwise specified, all works shall be part of the Contract lump sum. The Contractor shall supply all materials and plant required under the Contract. Materials shall meet the requirements of the relevant VicRoads Standard Specifications.

This section cross-references Sections 407,708, 709, 710, 714, 721, 811, 812, and 853, which are included in this contract.

750.A02 SCOPE OF WORKS

(a) The Contractor shall unless otherwise specified inspect, maintain and report on each pavement, roadside and structure infrastructure feature to the specified Intervention Criteria for the specified functional requirements.

(b) The Contractor shall unless otherwise specified provide an emergency response capability to assist with emergency incidents on the road network.

(c) Routine Maintenance Strategy

The Contractor shall implement an appropriate maintenance strategy, incorporating inspections, programming and work activities of both a pro-active and reactive nature to:

(i) ensure public safety;

(ii) meet the intervention criteria to minimise the rate of deterioration of the infrastructure;

(iii) sustain the functional requirements of each infrastructure feature; and

(iv) recognise and respond to the requirements of road users.


750.A03 DEFINITIONS

The following definitions shall apply:

(a) Clear Zone

An area constructed and/or landscaped in the road reserve to provide an additional recovery area for vehicles that leave the sealed carriageway at normal highway speed.

(b) Critical Location

A location where the road alignment and/or pavement width and/or geometry are identified by additional markings or furniture to guide the travelling public (cars, trucks, motorcycles, bicycles, and pedestrians).

(c) Defect

The visible or measurable evidence of failure or other undesirable condition.

(d) Failed pavement

Failed pavement is defined as:

• deformation greater than 25 mm in depth, measured with a 1.2 m straightedge transverse, or a 3 m straightedge longitudinal and where one or more of the following defects exist -

a. cracking greater than 50% of the area as defined in RM114(c) "Crocodile Cracking";

b. cracking greater than 5 lineal metres per square metre of pavement as defined in RM113 Crack Sealing;

c potholing as defined in RM111 Pothole Patching;

d shoving;

e. pumping of pavement material;

f. delamination;

g. corrugations;

• pavement that is unstable or visually moving under traffic;

• potholes that have been repaired more than twice.

(e) Flushed/Bleeding Surface

Where there is surplus binder on the road surface which is slippery in wet or frosty weather or becomes soft in hot weather.

(f) Functional Requirements of Maintenance

The functional requirements of each infrastructure feature are detailed in Table 750.A051.

(g) Hazard

An event, condition or substance which has the potential to cause harm to the health and safety of persons.

(h) Infrastructure Feature

A functional grouping of Standard Jobs which describes a particular feature of the road infrastructure, refer Table 750.A041.

(i) Intervention Criteria

The condition level at which a defect is recorded and either rectified or scheduled for rectification within a given response time.


(j) Litter/Debris

Any cardboard, paper, refuse, rubbish, glass, metal, hypodermic needle, rubber, tyre, vehicle part, dirt, waste matter, dead animals, vegetative matter or anything of a like nature greater than 75 mm and anything likely to cause damage to vehicles or otherwise be a hazard to the public or is visually intrusive.

(k) Non-road Infrastructure

Infrastructure in, on, under or over a road which is not road infrastructure, e.g. utilities.

(l) Rural Area

In relation to an Arterial Road, an area that is not an Urban Area.

(m) Other Paved Areas

Any paved areas other than the through carriageways and may include footpaths, medians and traffic islands. Paving may include, but not be limited to concrete, brick paving or asphalt surfacing.

(n) Pavement

Any area of the road formation that includes a sealed surface, unsealed surface, shoulder and drainage.

(o) Response Code

A code, designated by a letter from 'A' to 'E', that indicates the control mechanism and response time for responding to a particular hazard on particular road.

(p) Response Time

The maximum time to respond to a defect, based on consideration of the defect type, risk and severity, as detailed in Table 750.EO23.

(q) Road Asset System

Database used by VicRoads to store and access inventory and condition information related to pavements, surfaces and structures.

(r) Road Infrastructure

(i) The infrastructure which forms part of a roadway, pathway or shoulder, including:

• structures forming part of the roadway, pathway or shoulder

• materials from which a roadway, pathway or shoulder is made

• the drainage system;

(ii) The infrastructure which facilitates the operation of the road e.g. traffic control signs, road embankments.

(s) Road Maintenance Category (RMC)

The nominated road maintenance category for each road determined according to an assessment of risk, taking into account factors such as road classification, road type and volume and type of traffic. Designated by a number from '1' to '6' and published in the VicRoads Register of Public Roads and the VicRoads Road Management Plan.

(t) Road Number

The identification number of the infrastructure as provided by the Superintendent e.g. Princes Highway 2510.


(u) Roadside

Any area within the road reserve other than the pavement e.g. median, grassland.

(v) Road Reserve

All of the land that is within the boundaries of a declared road.

(w) Roadway

The area of the road that is used for the driving or riding of vehicles (including bicycles) but does not include driveways or other private access roads from adjoining land.

(x) Road User

Road users include cyclists, drivers of vehicles, passengers in vehicles, pedestrians using the road or road workers.

(y) Routine Maintenance

The regular program of activities including, but not limited to, inspections, repairs, works and associated reports designed to address the requirements of Clause 750.A02(c).

(z) Safety Sign

A road sign that provides the driver with advice about the safe use of a road.

(aa) Standard Job

Description of activity to restore and/or repair a component of an infrastructure feature element to a defect-free condition. A list of Standard Jobs is detailed in Table 750.A041.

(ab) Structure

PART 750.B - PAVEMENT MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS

750.B01 RM100 SEALED SURFACE

(a) General

Maintenance of sealed pavement shall be carried out in accordance with the RM100 series of standard jobs and RM211 in Table 750.H011.

HP Maintenance treatments on bituminous surface pavements and shoulders shall be submitted for review by the Superintendent prior to commencing work, including the type and mix of proposed materials. Unless otherwise approved by the Superintendent, material that is of a similar type to the existing surface shall be used. New pavement material shall be consistent with the existing pavement material and shall comply with Sections 811, 812 and 407. Where proposed, the Contractor shall submit for review the types of treatment for stabilised patching. Pavement repair treatments using a primerseal surface shall provide for a final seal surface re-treatment. All primerseals applied between July and the following June of any year shall have the final seal applied between the months of December and February of the following year.

The use of cold mix asphalt shall be restricted to emergency works. Unless otherwise approved by the Superintendent, patching carried out using cold mix asphalt shall be replaced as soon as practical with the appropriate approved treatment.

Treatments shall ensure that the finished surface matches the texture of the surface and shape of the pavement or shoulder. The surface level of any treatment shall not vary from the existing surface level by more than 10 mm under a 3 metre straight edge. Pre-existing line marking, road markings and raised reflective pavement markings shall be immediately reinstated after any repair.

Pavement joints created by maintenance treatments in bituminous and concrete surfaced pavements and shoulders shall be sealed immediately to prevent the ingress of water.

Loose aggregate resulting from sealing of patching, regulation, crack sealing or surface treatments shall be removed from the road pavement surface within 48 hours of completion of the seal, crack sealing or surface treatment. The Contractor shall maintain appropriate signage to warn motorists of the loose aggregate until it has been removed.

Any residual grit on the roadway surface from spills or other remedial works shall be removed prior to any speed restrictions being removed and the road deemed to be in normal operational use.

(b) Specific Requirements

RM113 Crack Sealing

Crack sealing will be undertaken in accordance with the special clauses as provisional works. The Superintendent shall nominate the sites to be treated by the Contractor. The work practices adopted by the Contractor shall be submitted to the Superintendent (as per 750.B01(a)).

RM131 Digouts

Further to RM131 - Digouts in Table 750.H011, treatment of failed areas shall be to excavate and replace failed pavement with the appropriate maintenance treatment.

The failed area shall be squared up to produce a regular shaped patch.

All digouts shall conform to the following performance criteria for surface materials, texture, line and level for a period of 6 months from the time of sealing.

(i) no point on the sealed surface of each digout shall lie more than 10 mm below a 3 metre straight edge laid in any direction; and

(ii) the treated area shall be free of cracking, flushing / bleeding and stripping.


The Contractor shall immediately rectify any digout that does not meet the performance criteria for surface materials, texture, line and level and all associated costs shall be borne by the Contractor. Unless otherwise approved, such repair shall be undertaken by either reconstruction of the patch or the placement of hot mix asphalt after primersealing.

Where digouts fail they shall be rectified by the Contractor at the Contractor's expense within the response time for RM131 in Table 750.H011.

750.B02 RM200 AND RM300 SHOULDERS AND UNSEALED SURFACES

(a) General

Maintenance of shoulders and unsealed pavement shall be carried out in accordance with RM221 and RM311 standard jobs in Table 750.H011. Where the shoulder is sealed in part width, the full width of the formed shoulder shall be maintained.

Further to Clause 750.A09 Limit of Works, in a rural area the shoulder is that portion of the carriageway beyond the traffic lanes to a line 200 mm inside the face of guide posts.

Maintenance treatments on shoulders and unsealed surfaces, including the type and mix of proposed material shall be submitted for review by the Superintendent prior to commencing work. Unless otherwise approved by the Superintendent, material that is of a similar type to the existing surface shall be used. New pavement material shall be consistent with the existing pavement material and shall comply with Sections 811 and 812.

Further to the requirements specified in Table 750.H011, the maintenance of unsealed pavements and shoulders shall include:

(i) regular attention to corrugations, potholes, depressions and scours;

(ii) maintenance of pavement and shoulder cross slopes;

(iii) attention to drainage to allow water to flow freely across the pavement and shoulder.

The Contractor is required to supply and place any additional material for the repair of unsealed shoulders and pavement.

Maintenance of sealed shoulders shall be in accordance with Section 750.B01.

(b) RM221 Unsealed Shoulder

When the shoulders along a sealed pavement are graded, care shall be taken to ensure that no damage is caused to the adjacent sealed surface. Any damage caused to the seal or to driveway accesses, shall be made good by the Contractor. Potholes and scours shall be repaired before grading.

Shoulder grading shall be in the direction of traffic and carried out in such a manner that loose or windrowed material is removed from the adjacent sealed pavement. Windrows shall be spread as soon as possible and, in any case shall not exceed 2 kilometres in length at any time, be completely spread and any material deposited on the adjacent sealed pavement is removed before work ceases for the day. The Contractor shall take care to ensure that stones are not graded onto the pavement. The surface of the graded shoulder shall be free of loose stones and compacted after grading to a smooth and uniform surface using a ##(minimum standard acceptable to be specified): tonne multi wheel roller.

Further to RM221 Unsealed Shoulder in Table 750.H011, unless otherwise specified, the Contractor shall maintain all unsealed shoulders to at least the crossfalls and widths existing at the commencement of the Contract.

Grading of unsealed shoulders can create a false drain in the vicinity of the line of the guideposts. The Contractor shall ensure that such a false drain does not result from any grading of unsealed shoulders.


Potholes or depressions in an unsealed pavement or shoulder shall be filled with material similar to that in the existing pavement or shoulder. Material shall be suitably compacted to give a smooth and uniform surface using a vibrating plate compactor or ##(minimum standard acceptable to be specified): tonne multi wheel roller as appropriate, and include no particles greater than 10 mm in size The surface of the new patch shall be level with or slightly above the surrounding surface.

750.B03 RM400 DRAINAGE

(a) General

Further to the requirements for drainage maintenance specified in the RM400 series standard jobs in Table 750.H011, the Contractor shall ensure that all drainage systems on the road reserve function to prevent:

(i) scouring of gravel or earth pavements, shoulders and drains;

(ii) softening of subgrade or soil formation causing failure or distortion of the pavement;

(iii) the pavement and subgrade becoming unstable due to excessive moisture and causing damage to the bitumen surface;

(iv) extensive land slips on both cuttings and embankments;

(v) flood and scour damage to adjacent buildings and land.

Where safety inspections are carried out during rain, the Contractor shall record and advise the Superintendent of areas where ponding of water occurs on the traffic lanes or shoulders.

Further to RM400 Drainage in Table 750.H011, drainage systems where there are known problem areas ## (specify known problem areas in a Contract Specific clause): shall be inspected after significant rainfall events. A significant rainfall event is defined where more than 25 mm of rain occurs in a one hour period as recorded at the closest Bureau of Meteorology weather station.

(b) RM411 Surface Drains and Verges

Drainage systems shall be maintained to allow the free flow of water from the pavement, across the shoulder and verge and into and along any longitudinal drain lines. Any build-up of material on the shoulder or verge that restricts the free flow of water shall be removed.

Care shall be taken to ensure that water is discharged in such a manner that there is no scouring. Ploughed fire breaks shall be closely watched for scouring and care taken to see that water is not carried from one catchment into the next.

The Contractor shall take the following precautions to protect the road and roadside from scour:

(i) keep all table, catch and diversion drains clear;

(ii) keep all existing scour protection work operational;

(iii) with the approval of the Superintendent, redirect concentrations of water from passing directly over shoulders and batters of cuttings and embankments;

(iv) maintain the growth of grass and other suitable vegetation on batters, the adjoining roadside and in drains on steep grades where scour is taking place;

(v) minimise disturbance of existing surface in areas prone to scour and use wide flat based drains;

(vi) ensure that all surface excavations are left so they will not scour;

(vii) maintain any chute drains.

(c) RM412 Sub-surface Drains

Further to requirements for RM412 Sub-surface Drains in Table 750.H011, flush and outlet points are usually marked by either one or more ceramic markers or a permanent stake. These markers shall be replaced as required. The outlet aprons and vermin guards of sub-surface drains shall be kept clean of weeds and the vermin guards securely in place and effective at all times.


(d) RM413 Culverts, Pit Cleaning and Stormwater Drains

Further to the requirements for RM413 - Culverts, Pit Cleaning and Stormwater Drains in Table 750.H011, pits shall be cleaned out and the culverts or pipes cleared of any obstructions. All material removed from pits and culverts shall be removed in accordance with Clause 750.G09.

(e) RM414 Culvert and Pit Repair

Further to the requirements for RM414 - Culvert and Pit Repair all repairs shall be carried out in accordance with VicRoads drawings and standard specifications.

(f) RM416 Kerb and Channel

Further to the requirements for RM416 - Kerb and Channel in Table 750.H011, sections of kerb and channel where water is not able to flow freely shall be reported to the Superintendent who may direct the repairs to be undertaken as provisional works or arrange it to be done by others.

750.B04 LAND SLIP CLEANING

Further to the requirements for RM813 - Land Slip Cleaning in Table 750.H011, the Contractor shall make himself aware of potential land slip sites and regularly inspect for the presence of slips and shall immediately notify the Superintendent of any signs of pending or present slips.

750.C02 CONTROL OF ROADSIDE VEGETATION

(a) RM511 Grass Mowing and RM512 Edge Trimming

(i) General

Further to the requirements in Table 750.H011 the Contractor shall ensure that cuttings from areas subject to mowing or edge trimming are not left on pavements, shoulders, kerb and channel or in drains and that there is no uncut grass between passes or around or under trees or road furniture. The Contractor is to ensure there is no excessive clumping of grass or windrowing following grass cutting.

Grass mowing and edge trimming to 3 m behind the line of guideposts shall include the mowing and trimming of any grass on the shoulder and the removal of grass from the sealed pavement. Where there are no guideposts mowing shall be to a width of 5 m from the edge of pavement, where practicable.

The Contractor shall include procedures for plant and equipment hygiene in the Environmental Management Plan.

Control of grass height using chemicals is not permitted unless approved by the Superintendent.

(ii) Cyclic Grass Cutting ##(strikethrough if performance based grass mowing is selected):

The Contractor shall submit a program of works, identifying the planned cyclic works for each of the roads specified.

(b) RM513 Grass and Weed Control

Further to the requirements in Table 750.H011, herbicide may be used to provide a vegetation free surface behind kerb or back of sealed shoulder, around fittings, furniture, culverts, bridge abutments, beaching and along fence lines and joints between pavement and kerb and channel.

Unless specified otherwise, spraying shall not exceed 200 mm from the back of kerb or edge of shoulder and 450 mm surrounding road furniture, guard fence or fencing.

(c) RM515 Tree and Shrub Management

(i) General

Trees growing within the road reserve shall not be cut down without the consent of the Superintendent. Fallen and damaged timber debris shall be removed from site by the Contractor when in urban area or freeway and from other roads when it interferes with mowing or impacts on other maintenance activities. Debris is required to be removed immediately where it poses a hazard to road users. Where a tree is removed by the Contractor the tree stump shall also be removed.

The Contractor shall instruct unauthorised persons cutting down trees or removing timber to stop. The person's name or the registration number of an accompanying vehicle shall be obtained and the occurrence immediately notified to the Superintendent.

Further to the requirements in Table 750.H011, tree and shrub pruning or removal shall be carried out in accordance with AS 4373-2007 - Pruning of amenity trees. PART 750.F - EMERGENCY RESPONSE

750.F01 GENERAL

Under the Emergency Management Act, VicRoads has statutory responsibility to meet emergency management obligations as:

  • the State Road Authority for Victoria;

  • a Support agency or the lead agency for Transport, Engineering and Services Support (TESS) under Victoria's State Emergency Response Plan (SERP).

The roles in routine emergency incidents on roads over which VicRoads exercises control include:

(a) providing manpower, equipment and materials to clear and/or make roads safe

(b) providing appropriate signing and traffic control assistance

(c) selecting and establishing appropriate alternative detour routes

(d) providing information to the public on road and traffic conditions.

When required under the provisions of the SERP, VicRoads provides assistance to control agencies which includes:

(i) fulfilling the same roles as for emergency incidents

(ii) assisting with the management of all road links, which can include emergency traffic management, escorting, route conditions and advice

(iii) providing support advice on transport and engineering matters which can include facilitating special transport needs and engineering advice such as the safety of bridges and land slips.

750.F02 CONTRACTOR REQUIREMENTS

(a) The Contractor is required to provide a 24 hour response capability to ensure safety of the public as a result of emergency incidents, including flooding, fires, storms and traffic crashes.

(b) The Contractor shall comply with a VicRoads' request for emergency response and make available any resources used in conjunction with this Contract. All directions and supervision for VicRoads Emergency Management Plan activities shall be through the Superintendent or advised by the VicRoads TMC and undertaken as Provisional Works.

(c) Control agencies for emergency response operations are designated in the Emergency Management Manual Victoria. Notwithstanding the provisions of the Contract the Contractor shall provide assistance to and follow the directions of any control agency.

(d) If a section of road is affected by an emergency incident, the Contractor shall provide appropriate assistance to members of the public.

The Superintendent will provide the Contractor with one copy of relevant VicRoads Emergency Management Plan documentation for use during the Contract.

Prior to commencement of work on-site, the Contractor shall provide the Superintendent with details of a single contact to be contactable 24 hours per day, and other contact numbers which will be used by the Contractor during any emergency response.

PART 750.G - MISCELLANEOUS

750.G01 WET WEATHER, FLOODING AND STORMS

During wet weather the Contractor shall pay particular attention to sections of road which are likely to be damaged or inundated by water to minimise damage, provide signs on inundated sections of road and clear waterways, pit entrances and culverts of obstructions and divert water from the roadway when necessary.

The Road Network shall be inspected after significant storm events. A significant storm event is defined where wind gusts are greater than 90 km/hr in any period as recorded at the closest Bureau of Meteorology weather station.

750.G02 FROSTY WEATHER AND SNOW ##(strikethrough if not required):

Where icy road conditions occur the Contractor shall place grit or sand on icy sections of road to make roads safe and erect temporary "skidding car" symbolic signs to warn motorists of the hazard.

The Superintendent or the VicRoads TMC shall be notified immediately of snowfalls which hinder traffic and require clearing.

750.G03 SPILLAGES

The Contractor shall ensure that the relevant fire fighting authority is notified of hazardous materials spillages.

Where spillage of chemicals or fuel is involved action shall be taken under direction from the control agency or the Environment Protection Authority to prevent spillage from entering watercourses. The Contractor shall identify materials spilled by the HAZCHEM symbols and ensure that the Contractor's personnel are suitably protected when handling hazardous material.

Road surfaces affected by spillages shall be treated to provide safe driving conditions by the Initial Emergency Response Unit. Treatments which exceed the capabilities of the Initial Emergency Response Unit shall be directed by the Superintendent.

750.G04 FIRE PREVENTION

Should a fire occur in the vicinity of roads being maintained under the Contract, the Contractor shall advise VicRoads TMC and endeavour to keep the roadway open and provide for the safe movement of traffic. The Superintendent shall be notified of the occurrence as soon as possible. If the Contractor's resources are required to assist in putting out the fire, time spent fire fighting shall be paid as provisional works.

The Contractor shall ensure that all activities undertaken by Contractor personnel which have a potential to initiate a fire are properly planned and supervised at all times.

The Contractor shall put out unattended roadside camp-type fires. The Contractor shall not light fires for clearing of rubbish, burning off, or any other purpose without first obtaining approval in writing from the Superintendent and any relevant permit to burn.

The Contractor shall observe Country Fire Authority Roadside Management Guidelines. Fire prevention measures, including removal of flammable material from around bridge abutments, culverts, sheds, etc. shall be done prior to the summer period. All flammable material within 9 metres of fireplaces shall be cleared away.

Grass mowing shall not be undertaken on days of Total Fire Ban. Except with written approval of the Superintendent, other plant and equipment shall not be used on days of Total Fire Ban. In all cases grass slashers, mowers, rotary hoes, burners on bitumen heaters and other plant and equipment shall not be used contrary to any regulations, or by-laws.


Before and during fire restriction periods the Contractor shall inspect and maintain spark arresters on all plant to ensure effective operation. Knapsack pumps, fully charged and in good condition, must be carried on all items of automotive plant and trucks during fire restriction periods.

750.G05 HAZARDS TO ROAD USERS

The Contractor shall take the following steps to safeguard road users:

(a) provide any resources, including signs and traffic controllers necessary to protect road users;

(b) if the person responsible can be located, ask them to remove any obstruction immediately;

(c) in cases of serious hazards which may remain overnight, notify the Superintendent who may direct action to be taken by the Contractor or arrange for Victoria Police or other parties to attend.

If the person responsible does not illuminate any hazard likely to remain overnight, the Contractor shall provide adequate signing and lighting to protect road users. The Contractor shall advise the Superintendent of the cost, together with the name and address of the person responsible for the hazard.

750.G06 DAMAGE TO ROAD OR NON-ROAD INFRASTRUCTURE

The Contractor shall carry out works under this Contract in such a manner as to not damage any road infrastructure belonging to another road authority or any non-road infrastructure located within the road reserve. Any damage is to be notified to the infrastructure owner and repaired at the Contractor's expense. Where the Contractor observes damage to road infrastructure belonging to another road authority or non-road infrastructure, the owner of that infrastructure is to be notified by the Contractor and the Superintendent informed.

750.G07 ROADS WITH RAIL CROSSINGS AND TRAM LINES ##(strikethrough if not required):

The company or agency operating the railway or tram facility is responsible for maintaining the road pavement within 2.1 metres each side of the outer rails for rail crossings and 0.5 metres each side of outer rails for tram lines.

750.G08 WATER FROM MAINS, CHANNELS, STREAMS, DRAINS AND/OR DAMS

When it is necessary to obtain water from mains, channels, streams drains or dams, the Contractor shall obtain permission from the responsible water authority and observe conditions laid down.

750.G09 MANAGEMENT OF SPOIL AND EXCESS ROAD MAKING MATERIAL

Drainage spoil, unwanted shoulder material, digout material and other non-recyclable roadmaking material is not to be deposited on the roadside except where previously agreed with the Superintendent or in accordance with Roadside Management Plans. Bituminous material is to be taken off-site in all instances unless otherwise approved.

All material removed off-site shall be disposed of in a manner and place approved by the Superintendent, including material containing invasive weed species.

750.G10 STOCK AND OTHER ANIMALS

(a) Unattended Stock

The Contractor shall ensure that unattended stock on the road reserve do not represent a hazard to road users. No action need be taken on stock tethered but unattended provided they do not represent a hazard to road users.

The Contractor shall immediately notify the Superintendent of any unattended stock. The Superintendent may direct the Contractor to remove the unattended stock from the road reserve under dayworks, or make arrangements for this to be done by others.


(b) Dead or Injured Animals

The Contractor shall dispose of dead animals from the pavement immediately, and elsewhere within the road reserve within 24 hours.

After removing any identification tags or markings the Contractor can dispose of small dead animals by burying within the road reserve or disposal off-site and arrange for larger dead animals to be disposed of off-site. Any disposal shall comply with relevant legislation and by-laws. Where an identification tag or marking enables the owner to be identified, the owner or Council shall be notified of the incident and the location of the animal.

If there are injured animals on the road reserve which should be destroyed the matter shall be reported immediately to Victoria Police and the Contractor shall dispose of any animals so destroyed.

Where dead animals, fish or offal being transported are left on the road as a result of a crash the owner of the vehicle is responsible for removal and clean up. The Contractor shall move off the carriageway whatever is necessary for the safe passage of road users. Where arrangements cannot be made for cleanup by others in a reasonable time the Contractor shall seek the approval of the Superintendent to carry out such work as dayworks.

(c) Pest Animals

The Contractor shall note through routine road inspections, and advise the Superintendent of areas of roadside observed as harbour for pest animals such as rabbits and foxes.

The Contractor shall also ensure that maintenance activities such as stockpiling of drainage material or other spoil do not become suitable sites for the establishment of pest animals.

750.G11 ABANDONED VEHICLES

Any vehicle that is left on an arterial road or freeway for a period greater than 48 hours is deemed to be abandoned.

With the approval of the Superintendent the Contractor may shift a vehicle which he has reasonable cause to believe has been abandoned and is a hazard to road users. The Contractor shall take care not to damage vehicles when moving them and arrange for Victoria Police to access locked vehicles.

For any vehicle which the Contractor suspects has been abandoned, the time, date, location and registration details shall be recorded and the Superintendent advised. If the Superintendent advises that the vehicle is stolen, the Contractor shall have no further involvement. If the vehicle is not stolen, the Contractor shall affix an "abandoned vehicle sticker"(provided by the Superintendent) to the vehicle 48 hours after the time of initial sighting and advise the Superintendent. The Superintendent is responsible for making arrangements for the removal of an abandoned vehicle.

Burnt out, stripped or remnants of vehicles abandoned on road reserves are to be regarded as scrap and shall be removed by the Contractor.

750.G12 PRIVATE ROAD STRUCTURES

Persons wanting to erect structures on road reserves must obtain the consent of the Superintendent. Any new letter boxes, signs or other structures erected in the road reserve shall be reported to the Superintendent.

750.G13 PRIVATE ENTRANCES

Any person wishing to construct a private entrance to a property must first obtain the consent of the Superintendent. Any new entrance shall be reported to the Superintendent.

The maintenance of the surfacing of a private entrance is the responsibility of the owner. The Contractor shall maintain all private entrance culverts as part of the lump sum.


750.G14 RESTORATION OF ROAD OPENINGS

Where road openings have been done by others, the Superintendent may direct that maintenance of any temporary repair or final restoration be carried out by the Contractor as provisional works. Final restoration work shall be carried out by the Contractor within seven (7) days of notification by the Superintendent of the road opening being completed.

The Contractor is to immediately notify the Superintendent of any road opening identified during a safety inspection that has a defect meeting the criteria listed in Table 750.E022.
