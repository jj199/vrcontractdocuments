This specification details the standards of performance required for routine maintenance of vegetation on roadsides. It also details inspection, emergency response and record requirements.

752.A03 DEFINITIONS

(a) Clear Zone

An area in the road reserve to provide an additional recovery area for vehicles that leaves the sealed carriageway at normal highway speed (at or below the regulatory speed limit).

(b) Critical Location

A location where the road alignment and/or pavement width and/or geometry is identified by additional markings or furniture to guide the travelling public (cars, trucks, motorcycles, bicycles, and pedestrians).

(c) Defect

The visible evidence of failure or other undesirable condition including the measured or observed condition of a standard job that meets or exceeds its specified intervention criteria as defined in Table 752.H011.

(d) Hazard

An event, condition, defect or substance which has the potential to cause harm to the health and safety of persons and meets or exceeds the Intervention Criteria as described in Table 752.B021.

(e) Hazard Reference Number

The reference number assigned to a type of Hazard as described in Table 752.B021. In most cases these correspond with the standard job numbers but are assigned an 'H' prefix rather than an 'RM' prefix.

(f) Intervention Criteria

The condition at which a Defect is recorded and either rectified or scheduled for rectification within the specified response time, unless otherwise agreed.

(g) Lineal km

The road length measured on one side of the carriageway.

(h) Litter/Debris

Any cardboard, paper, refuse, rubbish, glass, metal, hypodermic needle, rubber, tyre, vehicle part, dirt, waste matter, dead animals, vegetative matter or anything of a like nature greater than 75 mm length, and anything likely to cause damage to vehicles or be a hazard to the public.


(i) Native Vegetation

Any Australian plant that occurs and grows naturally in a specific region or locality, and includes trees, shrubs and ground covers (including grasses).

(j) Operational Works Maintenance Agreement

Agreements entered into between VicRoads and Council to maintain designated roadside areas within the road reserve for which they are not the Responsible Road Authority.

(k) Other Paved Areas

Any paved areas other than the through carriageways and may include footpaths, medians and traffic islands. Paving may include, but not be limited to concrete, brick paving, synthetic grass, granitic sand or asphalt surfacing.

(l) Pavement

Any area of the roadway that includes a sealed surface or unsealed surface.

(m) Response Time

The maximum time to respond to a Hazard or Defect, based on consideration of the Defect type, risk and severity, as detailed in Table 752.B021 and Table 752.H011.

(n) Road Asset System

Database used by VicRoads to store and access inventory and condition information related to pavements, surfaces and structures.

(o) Road Formation

Any area that includes roadway, shoulder, verge, batter, drain and within 1 m of the drain invert.

(p) Road Maintenance Category (RMC)

The nominated road maintenance category for each road determined according to an assessment of risk, taking into account factors such as road classification, road type and volume and type of traffic. Designated by a number from '1' to '6' and published in the VicRoads Register of Public Roads.

(q) Road Management Plan

A Road Management Plan in accordance with the Road Management Act 2004, establishes good road asset management practices to maintain a safe and efficient road network suitable for all members of the public in various traffic conditions.

(r) Road Number

The identification number of the road as provided by the Superintendent e.g. Calder Highway 2530.

(s) Roadside

Any area within the road reserve other than the pavement e.g. median, grassland.

(t) Road Reserve

All of the land that is within the boundaries of a declared road.

(u) Roadway

The area of the road that is used for the driving or riding of vehicles (including bicycles) but does not include driveways or other private access roads from adjoining land.


(v) Road User

Anyone using the road reserve and may include cyclists, drivers of vehicles, passengers in vehicles, pedestrians using the road or road workers.

(w) Routine Maintenance

The regular program of activities including, but not limited to, inspections, repairs, works and associated reports designed to address the requirements of this specification.

(x) Routine Maintenance Component

A component of routine maintenance, comprising pavement, roadside or structure maintenance as detailed in Table 752.A041.

(y) Routine Maintenance Sub-Component

A component of pavement, roadside or structure maintenance, and comprising a group of standard jobs as detailed in Table 752.A041.

(z) Rural Area

In relation to an Arterial Road, an area that is not an Urban Area.

(aa) Safety Sign

A road sign that is a Regulatory sign or a sign that provides the driver with advice about the safe use of a road.

(ab) Standard Job

Description of activity to make safe, restore and/or repair a pavement, roadside or structure component of routine maintenance to a Defect-free condition. A list of Standard Jobs is detailed in Tables 752.A021, 752.A022 and 752.A023.

(ac) Structure

A bridge, culvert or sign gantry or other designated structure, which has usually been assigned an asset structure number under VicRoads Bridge Management System.

(ad) Tree and Shrub Plantation (Plantations)

An area of roadside that has been specifically planted or developed with plants for the amenity enhancement or for other reasons of safety, screening etc. of the roadside and which the surface of the planted and surrounding area may be grassed, not grassed, mulched or matted.

(ae) Unit of Work

Base unit of measurement of a standard job as defined in Table 752.H011.

(af) Urban Area

In relation to an Arterial Road, an area in which:

(i) a speed limit of 60 km/hour or less applies, not being a speed limit which applies only because of a temporary reason such as roadworks or a street event; or

(ii) there are buildings on land next to the road, or there is street lighting, at intervals not exceeding 100 m for -

- a distance of at least 500 m; or

- if the length of the road is less than 500 m, over the length of the road.


(ag) Vegetation Seedlings

Naturally established trees and shrubs less than 1.5 m in height.

(ah) Vegetation Regrowth

New vegetative growth from trees and shrubs which have been previously pruned or cut down.

752.A05 FAILURE TO COMPLY WITH SPECIFIED STANDARDS AND PROGRAM OF WORKS

Where in the Superintendent's opinion, reasonably held and based on monthly observations over a three-month period, the Contractor fails to satisfy one or more of the following:

(a) adequately complete Monthly Defect Inspections to achieve at least 85 per cent compliance with any audit conducted by the Superintendent in accordance with Clause 752.B04; or

(b) adequately submit Monthly Works Programs in accordance with Clause 752.B03; or

(c) undertake and complete works identified in Hazard Inspections and requiring a response in accordance with Clause 752.B02; or

(d) undertake and complete at least 95 per cent of the Defect items listed on the relevant monthly Works Program, for standard jobs other than those requiring a response in accordance with Clause 752.B02 and which are paid under the Routine Maintenance component of the Contract, as shown in Table 752.H011; or


(e) undertake and complete Provisional Works in accordance with the specified requirement or standards and within the specified times; or

(f) record or provide the information specified;

the Superintendent may:

(i) withhold from the monthly claim, the value of the uncompleted works, as assessed by the Superintendent; and/or

(ii) utilise other Contractors' resources to carry out these works with the actual cost of doing so (including VicRoads administration) being deducted from the Contractor's monthly payment; and/or

(iii) deduct an amount from the monthly claim equating to the non-compliance of not completing at least 95 per cent of the agreed monthly programmed works for standard Routine Maintenance works other than those requiring a response in accordance with Clause 752.B02, for example, if the Contractor completes only 80 per cent of the monthly programmed Routine Maintenance works, then a 20 per cent deduction to the Schedule Item for Routine Maintenance shall be applied; and/or

(iv) deduct an amount from the monthly claim equating to the non-compliance of not adequately completing Monthly Defect Inspections to achieve at least 85 per cent compliance with an audit compiled by a joint inspection conducted in accordance with Clause 752.B04, for example, if the Contractor identifies only 80 per cent of the quantity of Defects identified in any joint inspection of Defects, then a 20 per cent deduction to the Schedule Item for Defect Inspections shall be applied; and

(v) where deductions have occurred on any 3 occasions within any 12 month period, this may be considered to be a default in performance by the Contractor, to which the General Conditions of Contract may be applied.

752.A07 TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT REQUIREMENTS FOR ROUTINE ROADSIDE MAINTENANCE WORKS

Further to the requirements in Clause 166.02 all Routine Maintenance works shall be undertaken in accordance with the Code of Practice for Worksite Safety - Traffic Management (RMA 2004 or the most recent version), and shall be defined as either:

(a) On-Road / Pavement Works - shall be assessed as Mobile Works, Very Short Term Works, Short Term Works or Long Term Works, whichever is the most appropriate. Mobile inspections shall be assessed as Very Short Term Works.


(b) Off-Road / Roadside Works (including grass mowing) that are undertaken from the pavement - shall be assessed as Very Short Term Works - Frequently Changing Works Area. The requirements of Figure 3 of the Code of Practice for Worksite Safety - Traffic Management (RMA 2004 or the most recent version) must be achieved for sight distance and adequate passing width for two way traffic.

Off-Road / Roadside works that are undertaken from the roadside can be undertaken as Very Short Term Works. Activities that may need some encroachment into the roadway that can be undertaken safely during gaps in traffic (e.g. a grass mower manoeuvring around an obstacle whilst mowing the roadside or median) shall be subject to a risk assessment. The minimum roadworks signing shall be in accordance with Figure 3 of the Code of Practice for Worksite Safety - Traffic Management (RMA 2004 or the most recent version).

Payment for all traffic management shall be deemed to be included in all work associated with General, Routine Maintenance, Programmed Maintenance and Provisional Quantity items. Payment for traffic management at Daywork Rates shall only occur as part of Provisional Sum works and only where approved by the Superintendent.

752.A08 LIMIT OF WORKS

The limits of works under this Contract shall be as defined in the Road Management Act 2004 Code of Practice - Operational Responsibilities for Public Roads, Section 100 of the Contract and including maps/lists/schedules of the Urban and Non Urban arterial roads and freeway classifications.

Any reference made to VicRoads responsibility as the responsible road authority, shall be interpreted as the Contractor's responsibility.

752.A9 NEW WORKS

VicRoads may from time to time install, or allow others to install assets such as signs, poles, guard fence, wire rope safety barrier, tree/shrub planting, shoulder upgrading works etc. within the areas to be maintained. The maintenance requirements associated with these new assets (where VicRoads will be responsible for ongoing maintenance) shall become part of this Contract, beyond any defects/maintenance liability period associated with their installation. The Contractor shall maintain these assets in accordance with Clause 752.A02.

752.A10 CERTIFICATES AND QUALIFICATIONS

The Contractor shall be required to hold and maintain for the duration of the contract, the following certificates and qualifications.

(a) All personnel involved in the storage, handling and application of herbicides shall hold appropriate licenses for such works, including but not limited to those required by the Public Health and Wellbeing Act 2008, and/or hold a Commercial Operators Licence (COL) issued by the Department of Environment, Land, Water and Planning.

(b) The Contractor shall have a staff member with a minimum of an advanced Certificate III in Horticulture, and/or relevant experience of at least 3 years. This person must be on site during all herbicide and garden bed maintenance activities with the exception of litter collection.

(c) The Contractor shall ensure that all staff members on site have an accredited certificate in plant identification for both native plant and weed species within Victoria.


PART B ‑ PROGRAMMING, MANAGEMENT AND RECORDS

752.B01 CONDITION SURVEYS

(a) Initial Condition Survey

The purpose of the Initial Condition Survey is to confirm, measure and record those standard jobs that meet or exceed the Intervention Criteria and require rectification, and thus provide a record of the condition of the road network at commencement of the Contract.

The Initial Defect Inspection undertaken and as described in Clause 752.B04 shall become the Initial Condition Survey for the Contract. This Initial Condition Survey shall be completed within the first month after the Contractor is given possession of the site and shall be a joint inspection involving representatives of the Contractor and the Superintendent, unless otherwise directed by the Superintendent.

Where as a result of the Initial Condition Survey, a normal routine standard job, apart from those items listed in Table 752.I021, is found to meet or exceed the specified intervention criteria, the Contractor shall repair or rectify the Defect within the specified response time or within the time period agreed with the Superintendent at the joint programming meeting. Payment for bringing Defects identified in the Initial Condition Survey back to the required standard shall be in accordance with the appropriate payment method specified in Table 752.H011 unless directed otherwise by the Superintendent.

(b) Road Network Condition Survey

Monthly Defect Inspections as described in Clause 752.B04, undertaken throughout the Contract duration, shall form the ongoing condition surveys for the road network included in the Contract.

(c) Contract Completion Condition Survey

The Defect Inspection as described in Clause 752.B04, undertaken 2 months prior to the Date of Practical Completion shall become the Contract Completion Condition Survey and shall be a joint inspection involving representatives of the Contractor and the Superintendent. Defect Inspections are still required after the Contract Completion Condition Survey.

Where standard jobs are identified as meeting or exceeding the specified Intervention Criteria the Superintendent may:

(i) direct the Contractor to repair or rectify the Defects within a period to be determined by the Superintendent; or

(ii) arrange for the rectification of those Defects by others and in this event a deduction will be made to the Contract Sum for the full cost of such works.

752.B03 MONTHLY WORKS PROGRAM AND JOINT PROGRAMMING MEETINGS

(a) To assist in the programming of maintenance works, and further to Clause 163.D1, the Contractor shall develop and maintain two Work Programs in a format acceptable to the Superintendent as follows:

(i) a 12 month Maintenance Program that identifies the Routine and Programmed Maintenance works for all roads within the Contract. The Contractor shall review and update the Maintenance Program on a 3 monthly basis and submit an updated Maintenance Program every 3 months illustrating the proposed maintenance works for the forthcoming 12 month period; and

(ii) a 3 month Look-ahead Program that identifies the Programmed Maintenance and other agreed maintenance works that are programmed for completion on all roads within the upcoming 3-month period. The program shall identify those maintenance jobs which meet or exceed the Intervention Criteria and are yet to be repaired or remedied. The Contractor shall include a plant, labour and material schedule that illustrates the expected resources necessary to complete the programmed maintenance activities. The Contractor shall review and update the Look-ahead Program each month.

The Superintendant may vary the frequency or scope of the Programmed Maintenance Works and if required to do so, shall advise the Contractor no later than 14 days prior to the programmed commencement to the Programmed Work.


Five business days prior to each monthly Site Meeting, the Contractor shall status the works achieved for the previous month against the proposed work for that month in the previous Look-ahead program and report on the percentage of programmed works that were satisfactorily completed. The report of completed works shall be used by the Contractor in developing the revised 3 month Look-ahead program for the next 3 month period.

Where the Contractor fails to develop the required Works Program, the Superintendent reserves the right to develop such Works Program at the Contractor's cost.

(b) Joint Programming Meetings may be held in conjunction with the monthly Site Meeting. The purpose of the Joint Programming Meeting shall be to:

(i) review the works completed in the previous month

(ii) review current Defect Inspection reports

(iii) identify works to be completed in the coming month under the Provisional Sum items and Provisional Quantity items

(iv) agree on the most efficient timing for completion of the scheduled works on a needs-basis and a geographical basis where possible

(v) review resources availability.

752.B04 DEFECT INSPECTIONS

Defect Inspections shall involve the Contractor driving the entire road network maintained by the Contractor and recording Defects, including all of the Defects meeting or exceeding the Intervention Criteria described in Table 752.H011. Defect Inspections of the entire road network maintained by the Contractor shall be undertaken monthly, for the duration of the Contract. For these monthly Defect Inspections, the Superintendent will inspect at least 10 per cent of the road network each month and the Contractor shall inspect the remainder of the road network in the same manner. The Superintendent will inspect different sections of the road network on each successive monthly inspection to ensure the Superintendent progressively inspects the whole of the road network. The Superintendent will provide the recorded Defect Inspection data to the Contractor for inclusion in the Contractor's Monthly Works Program.

HP Prior to commencing Defect Inspections, the Contractor shall submit to the Superintendent for review, a works method statement detailing how the Defect Inspections are to be conducted.

Data from Defect Inspections collected by the Contractor and the Superintendent shall be compared (in the context that equivalent sections are inspected by the Superintendent and the Contractor, one month apart) and collated, and the Contractor shall provide a record of Defect Responses to the Superintendent at a frequency not exceeding one month. It shall be in electronic format using the standard ASCII character set, or some other form agreed by the Superintendent. Each data record shall be formatted in a single line with data items separated by commas and all text enclosed in double quotation marks.

The Contractor is required to achieve in the Contractor's monthly Defect listings, at least 85 per cent compliance with the quantity of those listed by the Superintendent. Although in monthly inspections, the Superintendent will inspect road sections different from those inspected by the Contractor, if after 3 consecutive monthly inspections it is apparent that the Contractor may not be identifying or recording Defect to a similar degree as the Superintendent, then the Superintendent may conduct a joint inspection of at least 10 per cent of the network inspected by the Contractor, to establish and remedy the disparity between the Contractor's Defect Inspections and the 85 per cent compliance requirement.


752.B05 INCIDENT REPORTING

(a) Traffic Crashes at Works Being Done by the Contractor, Damage to VicRoads Property

(i) When any traffic crash occurs at works being done by the Contractor, the Contractor shall immediately notify the occurrence to the Superintendent.

(ii) Further to Clause 752.B05(a)(i), the Contractor shall submit a written report to the Superintendent in a format acceptable to the Superintendent within 24 hours of the Contractor being aware of an incident where any of the following are involved:

- VicRoads property is damaged;

- a traffic crash occurs on a section of road on which work is being carried out by the Contractor;

- persons interfere with roadside vegetation and/or VicRoads property; or

- any incident involving WorkSafe Victoria.

The report shall include:

- details of the crash scene showing the location of all signs (including wording), all lights, pavement markings, pavement condition, etc.;

- where any vehicle hits a roadside tree or pole, the distance of the tree or pole from edge of pavement shall be measured and included in the report;

- where traffic crashes occur on a section of road on which work is being carried out by the Contractor or where the Contractor's vehicles are involved, the position of wheel marks of vehicles measured from the edge of the road at intervals of 6 metres along the road for sufficient distance to show the movements of the vehicles before and after the crash, shall be drawn on a plan. The distance along the road shall be measured from some fixed point such as a culvert or kilometre marker;

- where damage has been caused to VicRoads property, this shall be clearly detailed in the crash report to the Superintendent. Where the information is available, the Contractor shall identify in the report the person responsible for damage to VicRoads property by supplying the name, address and telephone number of the person, or the make and registration of the vehicle involved in the incident.

(b) Notice of Incident

If a person proposes to make a claim in relation to an incident arising out of the condition of a public road, the person must give written notice of the incident to the responsible road authority. When VicRoads receives a Notice of Incident in relation to a section of road under the Contractor's control and a 'Condition Report' is required to be completed, then a copy of the Notice of Incident and a part completed Condition Report will be forwarded to the Contractor.

The Contractor shall supply to the Superintendent within seven days of receiving the Notice of Incident a summary of all inspections, maintenance and repairs conducted by the Contractor in the 12 months period before the date of the incident and any other relevant information available to the Contractor to enable completion of the Condition Report by the Superintendent.

(c) Environmental Incident

The Contractor is required to report to the Superintendent any environmental incidents which could affect the road reserve (e.g. spills, dumping, native vegetation clearance).

PART D ‑ ROADSIDE MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS

752.D01 RM500 VEGETATION.

(a) General

Maintenance of vegetation shall be carried out in accordance with Road Maintenance Sub-Component RM500 in Table 752.H011.

All works carried out on the roadside shall be undertaken to prevent or minimise any disturbance to native or other planted vegetation or fauna. The Contractor shall comply with:

(i) Victorian State Government Planning Provisions relating to Native Vegetation Retention, Environmental Protection and Conservation

(ii) maintenance requirements contained within the VicRoads Regional Roadside Conservation Management Plan, the VicRoads Roadside Handbook and strategic Fire Management Plans

(iii) VicRoads Regional Code of Practice

(iv) protection of landmarks and points of historic/cultural interest

(v) all clauses for any maintenance of water sensitive roadside features.

Unless otherwise specified, native vegetation, including native grasses and other ground cover species shall not be disturbed. The Contractor shall be responsible for the reinstatement of any roadside areas that have been damaged by the Contractor as a result of any maintenance activities, including herbicide spraying.

(b) Roadside Maintenance in Urban Areas

Unless otherwise specified:

(i) the Contractor is responsible for undertaking roadside maintenance activities on roadside areas including maintenance of above ground water sensitive road design features such as swale drains as defined in Section 100;

(ii) the limits of grass mowing for RM511 - Grass Mowing shall be as described under (a) or (d) of the Standard Job Description in Table 752.H011, as applicable.

(c) Roadside Maintenance in Non Urban (Rural) Areas

Unless otherwise specified:

(i) the Contractor is responsible for undertaking roadside maintenance activities on roadside areas including maintenance of above ground water sensitive road design features such as swale drains as defined in Section 100;

(ii) the limits of grass mowing for RM511 - Grass Mowing shall be as described under (c) or (d) of the Standard Job Description in Table 752.H011, as applicable.

(d) Roadside Maintenance on Freeways

Unless otherwise specified:

(i) the Contractor is responsible for undertaking roadside maintenance activities on roadside areas including maintenance of above ground water sensitive road design features such as swale drains as defined in Section 100;

(ii) the limits of grass mowing for RM511 - Grass Mowing shall be as described under (b) or (d) of the Standard Job Description in Table 752.H011, as applicable.

(e) Roadside Works by Others

Unless otherwise specified, works carried out by others on the road reserve, for example Adopt a Roadside schemes, do not relieve the Contractor of any specified maintenance requirements.


752.D02 CONTROL OF ROADSIDE VEGETATION

(a) RM511 Grass Mowing and RM512 Edge Trimming

(i) General

Further to the requirements as described in Table 752.H011 the Contractor shall ensure that cuttings from areas subject to mowing or edge trimming are not left on pavements or kerb and channel and that there is no uncut grass between passes or around trees or under road furniture. The Contractor is to ensure there is no excessive clumping of grass or windrowing following grass cutting.

Grass mowing and edge trimming to 3m behind the line of guideposts shall include the mowing and trimming of any grass on the shoulder, the removal of grass from the sealed pavement, and the removal of grass from around Culverts and Bridge Abutments. In Rural Areas, where there are no guideposts, mowing shall be to a width of 4.5 m from the edge of pavement. Grass mowing to a narrower width, will be acceptable, if there are fixed objects, such as trees, that prevent mowing to the required width with a reach mower, or there is identified native vegetation which is not to be mown.

Any pits, sign posts, guideposts and other road furniture damaged by the Contractor due to grass mowing activities shall be repaired or replaced by the Contractor at the Contractor's expense. In this regard, it shall be the Contractor's responsibility prior to grass mowing, to advise the Superintendent of known damage caused by others to pits, sign posts, guideposts and other road furniture.

The Contractor shall include procedures for plant and equipment hygiene in the Environmental Management Plan.

Control of grass height using chemicals is not permitted unless approved by the Superintendent.

(ii) RM511 Programmed Grass Mowing

The Contractor shall submit to the Superintendent for agreement, annual programs of works for grass cutting for each of the roads specified.

The estimated number of cuts per annum specified in Table 752.H011 under RM511 shall be undertaken as Programmed Maintenance and paid according to the number of approved cuts which will be dependant on weather conditions and seasonal grass mowing requirements.

(iii) RM512 Edge Trimming

Cut to back of kerb or pavement edge where no kerb and channel.

(b) RM513 Grass and Weed Control

Further to the requirements in Table 752.H011, herbicide may be used to provide a vegetation free surface on the road, behind kerb or back of sealed shoulder, around fittings, furniture, on or adjacent to concrete barriers, culverts, bridge abutments, beaching, paving, along urban freeway fence lines/sound walls and joints between pavement and kerb and channel.

Unless specified otherwise, spraying shall not exceed 200 mm from the back of kerb or edge of shoulder and 450 mm surrounding road furniture, guard fence or fencing.

(c) RM515 Tree and Shrub Management

(i) General

Trees growing within the road reserve shall not be cut down without the consent of the Superintendent. Fallen and damaged timber debris shall be removed from site by the Contractor when in an urban area or freeway and from other roads when it interferes with mowing or impacts on other maintenance activities. Debris is required to be removed immediately where it poses a hazard to road users. Where a tree is removed by the Contractor the tree stump shall also be removed.


The Contractor shall instruct unauthorised persons cutting down trees or removing timber to stop and the occurrence immediately reported to the Superintendent.

Further to the requirements in Table 752.H011, tree and shrub pruning or removal shall be carried out in accordance with AS 4373‑2007 - Pruning of amenity trees. Foliage which interferes with the maintenance of open drains, sight lines from private driveways and access roads or reduces the sight distance to traffic signals, road safety or other signs is to be trimmed.

Vegetation seedlings shall not be allowed to develop within the road formation, at bridges and culvert sites or where there is the potential to interfere with road furniture, between table drain and pavement when table drain is within 6 m of shoulder/pavement and also including the area 3 m behind wire rope safety barriers, or 2 m behind guard fence.

Vegetation seedlings shall be removed from within clear zones, where the clear zone has been created as part of a specific road safety program or where vegetation has been planted as part of a new project and where clear zone guidelines have been implemented.

Where trees from private property overhang the road reserve to such an extent that they affect road users, obscure signs or interfere with maintenance work, the Contractor shall advise the Superintendent prior to carrying out pruning work.

Where trees overhang from the road reserve into private property and a request from the land owner is made to remove them, the Contractor shall advise the Superintendent.

The Contractor shall be responsible for the replacement of trees or shrubs which die or are damaged as a result of poor herbicide application or poor maintenance techniques including mowing or brush cutting. Replacement plants are to be supplied by the Contractor at the Contractor's expense and shall be in 15 cm pots and a minimum of height of 300 mm. Planting shall be carried out at the Contractors expense and shall include re-mulching/matting and watering to maintain healthy growth. No planting shall occur between the months of October and April of any year.

The Contractor shall maintain records and provide details to the Superintendent of the number, type and size of trees removed at a frequency not exceeding 3 months. This data is required for DSE under the Native Vegetation Removal Exemption regulations

752.D03 USE OF HERBICIDES

HP Prior to the commencement of herbicide spraying, the Contractor shall submit a full list of herbicides intended for use on works under the contract, together with evidence that the personnel handling and applying the herbicides are certified to do so.

The Contractor shall handle and apply herbicides in accordance with the manufacturer's recommendations and ensure that all personnel handling and applying herbicides comply with relevant legislation regulating the use of herbicides.

Notwithstanding any other requirements stated in the Contract or stated by the manufacturer of the herbicides used, the Contractor shall not undertake herbicide spraying when prevailing weather conditions are likely to cause spray drift and/or vapour drift into adjacent properties and/or to cause harm to human life, stock or native vegetation and/or to cause runoff into adjacent waterways.

No herbicide spraying shall occur in any areas within the extent of works where the adjacent land use includes organically farmed products, vineyards or other similar activity where the type of herbicide needs to be carefully chosen, without the prior approval of the Superintendent.


All costs associated with the rectification of damage caused by the Contractor's application of herbicides shall be borne by the Contractor.

Unless specified otherwise, marker dyes shall be used in conjunction with all spraying.

The Contractor shall keep records of all herbicide used as specified in the Agricultural and Veterinary Chemicals (Control of Use) Regulations 1996. A copy of these records must be made available to the Superintendent when requested. The Contractor shall also keep records of all weeds treated together with approximate locations.

Copies of these records shall also be available for inspection by the Department of Human Services, Department of Environment, Land, Water and Planning and VicRoads for at least 3 years at the Contractor's workplace.

752.D04 RM822 LITTER CONTROL

The Contractor shall collect and dispose of all litter within the road reserve, including litter in tree and shrub beds, according to the specified Intervention Criteria. The Contractor is required to collect litter immediately prior to mowing so that no litter is cut or spread during grass mowing activities. The Contractor shall dispose of the litter at a registered landfill site.

Payment for the collection of litter in mown grass areas and plantations will form part of the grass cutting or plantation maintenance rate submitted for each road under the Contract. No separate payment will be made for the collection of litter in mown grassed areas and plantations when programmed work is undertaken.

Where a deposit of litter or rubbish greater than 1 m³ has occurred, or where public complaints regarding specific items of litter are received by either the Contractor or the Superintendant, that are not the result of the Contractor's failure to remove litter, the Contractor shall notify the Superintendent to seek approval to remove the litter as Provisional Works.

The Contractor shall immediately notify the Superintendent of any incidents of littering within the road reserve. If possible, the Contractor shall obtain the name and address and vehicle registration number of any person observed placing or dumping litter on the reserve and report these to the Superintendent. That person should be asked to remove the litter.

Where hazardous materials are found, the Superintendent shall be notified for direction as to the required action. Removal of hazardous materials shall be undertaken as Provisional Works. The Superintendent will, in consultation with the Contractor, nominate and approve the hazardous materials to be removed by the Contractor, or be done by others.

The Contractor shall inspect, and maintain rest areas, wayside stops and truck stops as described in Table 752.H011 as either Routine or Programmed Maintenance. The Contractor shall be responsible for the disposal of all needles and syringes deposited in rest areas. These needles and syringes shall be disposed of via an approved medical waste service.

For those rest areas where the boundary is clearly defined, RM820(A) Litter Collection and RM820(B) Mowing and Maintaining Grass Areas, shall be maintained to the specified Intervention Criteria to the boundary of the rest area.

At rest areas where the limits of the rest area are not clearly defined, RM820(A) Litter Collection and RM820(B) Mowing and Maintaining Grass Areas, shall be maintained to the specified Intervention Criteria for a minimum distance of 20 m from all paved areas and facilities, or if less than 20 m, as far as can be practically maintained.

PART G ‑ MISCELLANEOUS

752.G01 FIRE PREVENTION

Should a fire occur in the vicinity of roads being maintained under the Contract, the Contractor shall advise VicRoads TMC and endeavour to keep the roadway open and provide for the safe travel of road users. The Contractor shall notify the Superintendent of the occurrence as soon as possible. If the Contractor's resources are required to assist in putting out the fire, and provided the fire was not caused by the Contractor, time spent fire fighting shall be undertaken as Provisional Works and only where approved by the Superintendent for appropriately trained and equipped operators.

The Contractor shall ensure that all activities undertaken by Contractor personnel that have a potential to initiate a fire are properly planned and supervised at all times.

The Contractor shall not light fires for clearing of rubbish, burning off, or any other purpose without first obtaining approval in writing from the Superintendent and any relevant permit to burn.

The Contractor shall observe Country Fire Authority Roadside Management Guidelines. Fire prevention measures, including removal of flammable material from around bridge abutments, culverts, sheds, etc. (refer Clause 752.D02(a)(i)), shall be done prior to the summer period. All flammable material within 9 m of fireplaces shall be cleared away.

Grass mowing shall not be undertaken on days with a fire danger rating of Extreme or Code Red . In all cases grass slashers, mowers, rotary hoes, burners on bitumen heaters and other plant and equipment shall not be used contrary to any regulations or by‑laws.

Before and during fire restriction periods the Contractor shall inspect and maintain spark arresters on all plant to ensure effective operation. Knapsack pumps, fully charged and in good condition, must be carried on all items of automotive plant and trucks during fire restriction periods.

752.G02 OTHER ASSETS WITHIN THE ROAD RESERVE

The Contractor shall carry out works under this Contract in such a manner as to not damage any assets located within the road reserve and belonging to another authority or person. Any damage caused by the Contractor is to be notified to the owner and repair costs borne by the Contractor.

752.G03 USE OF WATER

When it is necessary to obtain water from mains, channels, streams drains or dams, the Contractor shall obtain permission from the responsible water authority and observe all conditions specified in the permit. Copies of permits shall be provided to the Superintendent upon request.

The Contractor shall maintain a record of volumes of all water usage including details of the source of the water and whether it is potable or non potable, its use and the cost of the water. A report shall be provided to the Superintendent every 3 months.


752.G04 STOCK AND OTHER ANIMALS

(a) Unattended Stock

The Contractor shall ensure that unattended stock on the road reserve do not represent a hazard to road users. No action need be taken on stock tethered but unattended, provided they do not represent a hazard to road users.

The Contractor shall immediately notify the Superintendent of any unattended stock.

(b) Dead or Injured Animals

The Contractor shall notify the Superintendent of any dead or injured animals within the road reserve. If there are injured animals on the road reserve which should be euthanized, the matter shall be reported immediately to Victoria Police.

(c) Pest Animals

The Contractor shall note through routine inspections, and advise the Superintendent of, areas of roadside observed as harbour for pest animals such as rabbits and foxes.

752.G05 ABANDONED VEHICLES

Any vehicle left on an arterial road or freeway for a period greater than 48 hours is deemed to be abandoned. The Contractor is to advise the Superintendent of any vehicle considered abandoned on the roadside.

752.G06 VANDALISM REPAIR

The Contractor shall report to the Superintendent the date, details and the extent of damage caused by vandals, including graffiti, within 24 hours of the Contractor being aware of the vandalism.
