750.A01 GENERAL

This specification details the performance standards required for Routine Maintenance of road pavements, roadsides and structures. It also details inspection, emergency response and record requirements.

The Contractor shall supply all materials and plant required under the Contract. Materials shall meet the requirements of the relevant VicRoads Standard Specifications.

This section cross-references Sections 407, 708, 709, 710, 714, 721, 811, 812 and 853 which are included in this Contract.

(a) Hazard Response

The Contractor shall conduct Hazard inspections to identify hazards, and remedy or make safe identified hazards within specified timeframes.

750.A03 DEFINITIONS

The following definitions shall apply:

(a) Clear Zone

An area in the road reserve to provide an additional recovery area for vehicles that leave the sealed carriageway at normal highway speed (at or below the regulatory speed limit).

(b) Critical Location

A location where the road alignment and/or pavement width and/or geometry is identified by additional markings or furniture to guide the travelling public (cars, trucks, motorcycles, bicycles, and pedestrians).

(c) Defect

The visible evidence of failure or other undesirable condition such that the measured or observed condition of a standard job meets or exceeds its specified intervention criteria as defined in Table 750.H011.

(d) Failed Pavement

● pavement with deformation greater than 30mm in depth for Road Maintenance Category 1, 2 and 3 roads or greater than 50 mm in depth for Road Maintenance Category 4 and 5 roads, under a 1.2 m straightedge and where one or more of the following exist -

(i) cracking greater than 50% of the area as described in Table 750.H011 RM114(b) 'Crocodile Cracking';

(ii) cracking of at least 2 mm width and greater than 5 lineal metres per 1 m x 1 m area of pavement;

(iii) potholing as defined in RM111 Pothole Patching;

(iv) shoving;

(v) pumping of material;

(vi) delamination; or

(vii) corrugations;

● pavement that is unstable or visually moving under traffic;

● potholes as described in Table 750.H011 RM111 or digouts/patches as described in Table 750.H011 RM131 that have been repaired more than twice.


(e) Flushing and/or Bleeding Surface

Where there is surplus binder on the road surface which is slippery in wet or frosty weather or becomes soft in hot weather.

(f) Hazard

An event, condition, defect or substance which has the potential to cause harm to the health and safety of persons and meets or exceeds the Intervention Criteria as described in Table 750.B022.

(g) Hazard Reference Number

The reference number assigned to a type of Hazard as described in Table 750.B022. In most cases these correspond with the standard job numbers but are assigned an 'H' prefix rather than an 'RM' prefix.

(h) Intervention Criteria

The condition at which a Defect is recorded and either rectified or scheduled for rectification within the specified response time, unless otherwise agreed.

(i) Lineal km

The road length measured on one side of the carriageway.

(j) Litter/Debris

Any cardboard, paper, refuse, rubbish, glass, metal, hypodermic needle, rubber, tyre, vehicle part, dirt, waste matter, dead animals, vegetative matter or anything of a like nature greater than 75 mm length, and anything likely to cause damage to vehicles or be a hazard to the public.

(k) Native Vegetation

Any Australian plant that occurs and grows naturally in a specific region or locality and includes trees, shrubs and ground covers (including grasses).

(l) Other Paved Areas

Any paved areas other than the through carriageways and may include footpaths, medians and traffic islands. Paving may include, but not be limited to concrete, brick paving or asphalt surfacing.

(m) Pavement

Any area of the roadway that includes a sealed surface or unsealed surface.

(n) Response Time

The maximum time to respond to a Hazard or Defect, based on consideration of the Defect type, risk and severity, as detailed in Table 750.B022 and Table 750.H011.

(o) Road Asset System

Database used by VicRoads to store and access inventory and condition information related to pavements, surfaces and structures.

(p) Road Formation

Any area that includes roadway, shoulder, verge, batter, drain and within one (1) metre of the drain invert.


(q) Road Maintenance Category (RMC)

The nominated road maintenance category for each road determined according to an assessment of risk, taking into account factors such as road classification, road type and volume and type of traffic. Designated by a number from '1' to '6' and published in the VicRoads Register of Public Roads.

(r) Road Management Plan

A Road Management Plan in accordance with the Road Management Act 2004, establishes good road asset management practices to maintain a safe and efficient road network suitable for all members of the public in various traffic conditions.

(s) Road Number

The identification number of the road as provided by the Superintendent e.g. Calder Highway 2530.

(t) Roadside

Any area within the road reserve other than the pavement e.g. median, grassland.

(u) Road Reserve

All of the land that is within the boundaries of a declared road.

(v) Roadway

The area of the road that is used for the driving or riding of vehicles (including bicycles) but does not include driveways or other private access roads from adjoining land.

(w) Road User

Anyone using the road reserve and may include cyclists, drivers of vehicles, passengers in vehicles, pedestrians using the road or road workers.

(x) Routine Maintenance

The regular program of activities including, but not limited to, inspections, repairs, works and associated reports designed to address the requirements of this specification.

(y) Routine Maintenance Component

A component of routine maintenance, comprising pavement, roadside or structure maintenance as detailed in Table 750.A041.

(z) Routine Maintenance Sub-Component

A component of pavement, roadside or structure maintenance, and comprising a group of standard jobs as detailed in Table 750.A041.

(aa) Rural Area

In relation to an Arterial Road, an area that is not an Urban Area.

(ab) Safety Sign

A road sign that is a Regulatory sign or a sign that provides the driver with advice about the safe use of a road.


(ac) Standard Job

Description of activity to make safe, restore and/or repair a pavement, roadside or structure component of routine maintenance to a Defect-free condition. A list of Standard Jobs is detailed in Table 750.A041.

(ad) Structure

A bridge, culvert or sign gantry or other designated structure, which has usually been assigned an asset structure number under VicRoads' Bridge Management System.

(ae) Unit of Work

Base unit of measurement of a standard job as defined in Table 750.H011.

(af) Urban Area

• a speed limit of 60 kilometres per hour or less applies, not being a speed limit which applies only because of a temporary reason such as roadworks or a street event; or

a distance of at least 500 metres; or

if the length of the road is less than 500 metres, over the length of the road.

(ah) Vegetation Regrowth

750.A05 FAILURE TO COMPLY WITH SPECIFIED STANDARDS AND PROGRAM OF WORKS

Where in the Superintendent's opinion, reasonably held and based on monthly observations over a 3 month period, the Contractor fails to satisfy one or more of the following:

(a) adequately complete Hazard Inspections in accordance with Clause 750.B02; or

(b) adequately complete Monthly Defect Inspections to achieve at least 85 per cent compliance with any audit conducted by the Superintendent in accordance with Clause 750.B04, or

(c) adequately submit Monthly Works Programs in accordance with Clause 750.B03, or

(d) undertake and complete works identified in Hazard Inspections and requiring a response in accordance with Clause 750.B02, or

(e) undertake and complete at least 95 per cent of the Defect items listed on the relevant monthly Works Program, for standard jobs other than those requiring a response in accordance with Clause 750.B02 and which are paid under the Routine Maintenance component of the Contract, as shown in Table 750.H011, or

(f) undertake and complete Provisional Works in accordance with the specified requirement or standards and within the specified times, or

(g) record or provide the information specified,

the Superintendent may:

(i) withhold from the monthly claim, the value of the uncompleted Routine Maintenance works, as assessed by the Superintendent; and/or

(ii) utilise other Contractors' resources to carry out these works with the actual cost of doing so (including VicRoads' administration) being deducted from the Contractor's monthly payment; and/or

(iii) deduct an amount from the monthly claim equating to the non-compliance of not completing at least 95 per cent of the agreed monthly programmed works for standard Routine Maintenance works other than those requiring a response in accordance with Clause 750.B02 (e.g. if the Contractor completes only 80 per cent of the monthly programmed Routine Maintenance works, then a 20 per cent deduction to the Schedule Item for Routine Maintenance shall be applied); and/or

(iv) deduct an amount from the monthly claim equating to the non-compliance of not adequately completing Monthly Defect Inspections to achieve at least 85 per cent compliance with an audit compiled by a joint inspection conducted in accordance with Clause 750.B04 (e.g. if the Contractor identifies only 80 per cent of the quantity of Defects identified in any joint inspection of Defects, then a 20 per cent deduction to the Schedule Item for Defect Inspections shall be applied); and

(v) where deductions have occurred on any three occasions within any 12 month period, this may be considered to be a default in performance by the Contractor, to which the General Conditions of Contract may be applied.

750.A07 TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT REQUIREMENTS FOR ROUTINE MAINTENANCE WORKS

Further to the requirements in Clause 166.02 all Routine Maintenance works shall be undertaken in accordance with the Code of Practice for Worksite Safety - Traffic Management (RMA 2004 or the most recent version), and shall be defined as either:

(a) On-Road / Pavement works - shall be assessed as Mobile Works, Very Short Term Works, Short Term Works or Long Term Works, whichever is the most appropriate. Mobile inspections shall be assessed as Very Short Term Works.

(b) Off-Road / Roadside works (including shoulder grading and grass mowing) that are undertaken from the pavement - shall be assessed as Very Short Term Works - Frequently Changing Works Area. The requirements of Figure 3 of the Code of Practice for Worksite Safety - Traffic Management (RMA 2004) must be achieved for sight distance and adequate passing width for two way traffic.

Off-Road / Roadside works that are undertaken from the roadside can be undertaken as Very Short Term Works. Activities that may need some encroachment into the roadway that can be undertaken safely during gaps in traffic (e.g. a grass mower manoeuvring around an obstacle whilst mowing the roadside or median) shall be subject to a risk assessment. The minimum roadworks signing shall be in accordance with Figure 3 of the Code of Practice for Worksite Safety - Traffic Management (RMA 2004).

Payment for all traffic management shall be deemed to be included in all work associated with General, Routine Maintenance, Programmed Maintenance and Provisional Quantity items. Payment for traffic management at Daywork Rates shall only occur as part of Provisional Sum works and only where approved by the Superintendent.

PART 750.B - PROGRAMMING, MANAGEMENT AND RECORDS

750.B01 CONDITION SURVEYS

(a) Initial Condition Survey

The purpose of the Initial Condition Survey is to confirm, measure and record those standard jobs that meet or exceed the Intervention Criteria and require rectification, and thus provide a record of the condition of the road network at commencement of the Contract.

The Initial Defect Inspection undertaken and as described in Clause 750.B04 and the Initial Night Hazard Inspection undertaken and as described in Clause 750.B02, shall become the Initial Condition Survey for the Contract. This Initial Condition Survey shall be completed within the first month after the Contractor is given possession of the site and shall be a joint inspection involving representatives of the Contractor and the Superintendent, unless otherwise directed by the Superintendent.

Where as a result of the Initial Condition Survey, a normal routine standard job, apart from those items listed in Table 750.I021, is found to meet or exceed the specified intervention criteria, the Contractor shall repair or rectify the Defect within the specified response time or within the time period agreed with the Superintendent at the joint programming meeting. Payment for bringing Defects identified in the Initial Condition Survey back to the required standard shall be in accordance with the appropriate payment method specified in Table 750.H011 unless directed otherwise by the Superintendent.

(b) Road Network Condition Survey

Monthly Defect Inspections as described in Clause 750.B04, undertaken throughout the Contract duration, shall form the ongoing condition surveys for the road network included in the Contract.

(c) Contract Completion Condition Survey

The Defect Inspection as described in Clause 750.B04, undertaken 2 months prior to the Date of Practical Completion shall become the Contract Completion Condition Survey and shall be a joint inspection involving representatives of the Contractor and the Superintendent. Hazard and Defect Inspections are still required after the Contract Completion Condition Survey.

Where standard jobs are identified as meeting or exceeding the specified Intervention Criteria the Superintendent may:

(i) direct the Contractor to repair or rectify the Defects within a period to be determined by the Superintendent; or

(ii) arrange for the rectification of those Defects by others and in this event a deduction will be made to the Contract Sum for the full cost of such works.

The response must be completed within the response time shown in Table 750.B022. Response times commence from the time of inspection or notification.

Where, because of the nature of the repair required, level of resources required or workload, it is not possible to rectify within the response time shown in Table 750.B022, the Contractor shall implement appropriate hazard warning within the response time shown in Table 750.B022 or 72 hours, whichever is the shorter period, until the repair can be completed. Appropriate warning includes, but is not limited to:

• provision of warning signs,

• traffic control action,

• diverting traffic around the site,

• installation of a temporary speed limit,

• lane closure,

• closure of the road to use by certain vehicle (e.g. a load limit),

• road closure, or

• any other measure which reasonably addresses the Contractor's duty of care to the road user.

Where the Contractor is unable to enact the response the Superintendent should be notified immediately in writing.

750.F01 GENERAL

Under the Emergency Management Act, VicRoads has statutory responsibility to meet emergency management obligations as:

  • the State Road Authority for Victoria;

  • a Support agency or the lead agency for Transport, Engineering and Services Support (TESS) under Victoria's State Emergency Response Plan (SERP).

The roles in routine emergency incidents on roads over which VicRoads exercises control include:

(a) providing manpower, equipment and materials to clear and/or make roads safe

(b) providing appropriate signing and traffic control assistance

(c) selecting and establishing appropriate detour routes

(d) providing information to the public on road and traffic conditions.

When required under the provisions of the SERP, VicRoads provides assistance to control agencies which includes:

(i) fulfilling the same roles as for emergency incidents

(ii) assisting with the management of all road links, which can include emergency traffic management, escorting, route conditions and advice

(iii) providing support advice on transport and engineering matters which can include facilitating special transport needs and engineering advice such as the safety of bridges and land slips.

PART 750.G - MISCELLANEOUS

750.G01 WET WEATHER, FLOODING AND STORMS

During wet weather the Contractor shall, to minimise damage, pay particular attention to sections of road which are likely to be damaged or inundated by water, and provide signs on inundated sections of road, and clear waterways, pit entrances and culverts of obstructions, and divert water from the roadway when necessary.

Where icy road conditions occur the Contractor shall place grit or sand on icy sections of road to make roads safe and erect temporary 'skidding car' symbolic signs to warn motorists of the hazard.

The Superintendent or the VicRoads TMC shall be notified immediately of snowfalls that hinder traffic and require clearing. Unless specified otherwise, the Contractor shall be responsible for clearing snow from the roadway to provide for safe travel by road users, and this work shall be undertaken as Provisional Works.

750.G03 RM813 LAND SLIP CLEANING

Further to the requirements for RM813 Land Slip Cleaning as described in Table 750.H011, the Contractor shall, as part of Hazard Inspections conducted in accordance with Clause 750.B02, inspect potential land slip sites that the Contractor becomes aware of and shall immediately notify the Superintendent of any signs of pending slips, or current slips exceeding 5 cubic metres.

All work to remove land slip material from the road pavement and table drain, up to 5 cubic metres quantity at each land slip incident, shall be undertaken as Routine Maintenance. The removal of land slip material where the quantity at each land slip incident exceeds more than 5 cubic metres shall be undertaken as Provisional Works. The Superintendent will, in consultation with the Contractor, nominate and approve the sites to be repaired by the Contractor, or be done by others.

The Contractor shall report to the Superintendent the date, details and the extent of damage caused by vandals, including graffiti, within 24 hours of the Contractor being aware of the vandalism.

Repair to vandalism, including removal or covering of graffiti, as described in Table 750.H011 shall be undertaken as Provisional Works. The Superintendent will, in consultation with the Contractor, nominate and approve the sites to be repaired by the Contractor, or be done by others.

750.G05 RM820 REST AREAS

The Contractor shall inspect, clean and maintain rest areas, wayside stops, truck stops and formal bus stops as described in Table 750.H011 as Routine Maintenance. Where litter bins, toilets, barbeques or seating facilities are provided at these sites they shall be kept in a clean and tidy condition. The Contractor shall arrange to empty litter bins and loose litter as described in Table 750.H011 and dispose of the contents at a registered landfill.


Where a deposit of litter or rubbish greater than 2 m³ has occurred that is not the result of the Contractor's failure to remove litter at the specified frequency, the Contractor shall notify the Superintendent to seek approval to remove the litter as Provisional Works using Daywork Rates.

The pavement area of all rest areas, wayside stops, truck stops and formal bus stops within the road reserve, vehicle parks and less official stopping locations adjacent to the road pavement shall be maintained by the Contractor to the required Intervention Criteria for the various activities, including the RM100 (Sealed Pavement) series, RM311 (Unsealed Road) and RM221 (Unsealed Shoulder).

At rest areas where the limits of the rest area are not clearly defined, RM820(a) Litter Collection and RM820(c) Mowing and Maintaining Grass Areas shall be maintained to the specified Intervention Criteria for a minimum distance of 20 metres from all paved areas and facilities, or as far as can be practically maintained with a push mower.

For those rest areas where the boundary is clearly defined, RM820(a) Litter Collection and RM820(c) Mowing and Maintaining Grass Areas, shall be maintained to the specified Intervention Criteria to the boundary of the rest area.

750.G06 RM822 LITTER CONTROL

The Contractor shall collect and dispose of all litter within the road reserve, including litter in tree and shrub beds, according to the specified Intervention Criteria, and shall dispose of the litter at a registered landfill. Litter control shall be undertaken as Routine Maintenance.

Where a deposit of litter or rubbish greater than 2 m³ has occurred that is not the result of the Contractor's failure to remove litter at the specified frequency, the Contractor shall notify the Superintendent to seek approval to remove the litter as Provisional Works using Daywork Rates.

The Contractor shall immediately notify the Superintendent of any incidents of littering within the road reserve. The Contractor shall obtain the name and address and vehicle registration number of any person observed placing or dumping litter on the reserve and report these to the Superintendent. That person should be asked to remove the litter.

Except where the Superintendent advises that litter will be removed by another person, litter shall be removed and disposed by the Contractor as Routine Maintenance.

Where hazardous materials are found, the Superintendent shall be notified for direction as to the required action. Removal of hazardous materials shall be undertaken as Provisional Works. The Superintendent will, in consultation with the Contractor, nominate and approve the hazardous materials to be removed by the Contractor, or be done by others.

750.G07 SPILLAGES

The Contractor shall ensure that the relevant fire fighting authority is notified of hazardous materials spillages.

Where spillage of chemicals or fuel is involved, action shall be taken under direction from the control agency or the Environment Protection Authority to prevent spillage from entering watercourses. The Contractor shall identify materials spilled, by the HAZCHEM symbols and ensure that the Contractor's personnel are suitably protected when handling hazardous material.

Road surfaces affected by spillages shall be treated by the Initial Emergency Response Unit to provide safe driving conditions. Treatments which exceed the capabilities of the Initial Emergency Response Unit shall be directed by the Superintendent.


750.G08 FIRE PREVENTION

Should a fire occur in the vicinity of roads being maintained under the Contract, the Contractor shall advise VicRoads TMC and endeavour to keep the roadway open and provide for the safe travel of road users. The Contractor shall notify the Superintendent of the occurrence as soon as possible. If the Contractor's resources are required to assist in putting out the fire, time spent fire fighting shall be undertaken as Provisional Works and only where approved by the Superintendent for appropriately trained and equipped operators.

The Contractor shall ensure that all activities undertaken by Contractor personnel that have a potential to initiate a fire are properly planned and supervised at all times.

The Contractor shall not light fires for clearing of rubbish, burning off, or any other purpose without first obtaining approval in writing from the Superintendent and any relevant permit to burn.

The Contractor shall observe Country Fire Authority Roadside Management Guidelines. Fire prevention measures, including removal of flammable material from around bridge abutments, culverts, sheds, etc. (refer Clause 750.D02(a)(i)) shall be done prior to the summer period. All flammable material within 9 metres of fireplaces shall be cleared away.

Grass mowing shall not be undertaken on days of 'Total Fire Ban'. In all cases grass slashers, mowers, rotary hoes, burners on bitumen heaters and other plant and equipment shall not be used contrary to any regulations or by‑laws.

Before and during fire restriction periods the Contractor shall inspect and maintain spark arresters on all plant to ensure effective operation. Knapsack pumps, fully charged and in good condition, must be carried on all items of automotive plant and trucks during fire restriction periods.

If grass mowing is to be undertaken during fire restriction periods, the Contractor shall on each day prior to the intended grass mowing, contact the relevant Country Fire Authority (CFA) Region between the hours of 1630 and 1700 to ascertain the forecast fire danger index as derived from the McArthur Mk 4 Grassland Fire Danger Meter CSIRO (modified) or as used by the CFA.

The following shall apply (unless otherwise specified by Superintendent):

(a) Forecast Fire Danger Index 0 to 20 - grass mowing is able to be carried out at the discretion of the operator.

(b) Forecast Fire Danger Index 20 to 27 - grass mowing is to be completed by 11:00am or at a completion time negotiated with the CFA regional Duty Officer if forecast conditions do not eventuate.

(c) Forecast Fire Danger Index greater than 27 - No grass mowing is to be undertaken, unless negotiated otherwise with the CFA regional Duty Officer if forecast conditions do not eventuate.

750.G09 HAZARDS TO ROAD USERS

When the Contractor becomes aware of the presence of objects or situations that may be hazardous, the Contractor shall take the following steps to safeguard road users:

(a) provide any resources, including signs, and traffic controllers necessary to protect road users;

(b) if the person responsible for the obstruction, can be located, ask the person to remove the obstruction immediately;

(c) in cases of serious Hazards which may remain overnight, notify the Superintendent who may direct action to be taken by the Contractor or arrange for Victoria Police or others to attend. If the person responsible does not illuminate any Hazard likely to remain overnight, the Contractor shall provide adequate signing and lighting to protect road users. The Contractor shall advise the Superintendent of the cost, together with the name and address of the person responsible for the Hazard.


750.G10 OTHER ASSETS WITHIN ROAD RESERVE

The Contractor shall carry out works under this Contract in such a manner as to not damage any assets located within the road reserve and belonging to another authority. Any damage caused by the Contractor is to be notified to the owner and repair costs borne by the Contractor.

750.G11 ROADS WITH RAIL CROSSINGS AND TRAM LINES

The company or agency operating the railway or tram facility is responsible for maintaining the road pavement within 2.1 metres each side of the outer rails for rail crossings and 0.5 metres each side of outer rails for tram lines.

All personnel working within 3 metres of rail lines must wear high visibility 'special purpose orange' coloured jackets.

All personnel working in the vicinity of railway lines should ensure that they do not wear red or green clothing. Also, they should not wear red or green hats, or carry red or green objects. These colours have definite meanings to all operations of the railway system for signalling and safe working, and should be avoided at all times.

750.G12 USE OF WATER

When it is necessary to obtain water from mains, channels, streams drains or dams, the Contractor shall obtain permission from the responsible water authority and observe all conditions specified in the permit. Copies of permits shall be provided to the Superintendent upon request.

The Contractor shall maintain a record of volumes of all water usage including details of the source of the water and whether it is potable or non potable, its use and the cost of the water. A report shall be provided to the Superintendent every 3 months.

750.G13 MANAGEMENT OF SPOIL AND EXCESS ROAD MAKING MATERIAL

Drainage spoil, unwanted shoulder material, digout material and other non-recyclable roadmaking material is not to be deposited on the roadside or on any stacksite except where previously agreed with the Superintendent or in accordance with Roadside Management Plans. Bituminous material is to be taken off-site in all instances unless otherwise approved.

All material removed off-site shall be disposed in a manner and place approved by the Superintendent, including material containing invasive weed species.

750.G14 STOCK AND OTHER ANIMALS

(a) Unattended Stock

The Contractor shall ensure that unattended stock on the road reserve do not represent a hazard to road users. No action need be taken on stock tethered but unattended provided they do not represent a hazard to road users.

The Contractor shall immediately notify the Superintendent of any unattended stock. The Superintendent may direct the Contractor to remove the unattended stock from the road reserve, or make arrangements for this to be done by others.

(b) Dead or Injured Animals

The Contractor shall dispose of dead animals from the road formation within the response times shown for RM822 Litter Control in Table 750.H011.


After removing any identification tags or markings the Contractor can dispose of small dead animals within the road reserve and arrange for larger dead animals to be disposed off-site. Any disposal shall comply with relevant legislation and by-laws. Where an identification tag or marking enables the owner to be identified, the owner or Council shall be notified of the incident and the location of the animal.

If there are injured animals on the road reserve which should be destroyed, the matter shall be reported immediately to Victoria Police and the Contractor shall dispose of any animals so destroyed.

Where dead animals, fish or offal being transported are left on the road as a result of a crash the owner of the vehicle is responsible for removal and clean up. The Contractor shall move off the carriageway whatever is necessary for the safe passage of road users. Where arrangements cannot be made for cleanup by others in a reasonable time the Contractor shall seek the approval of the Superintendent to carry out such work as Provisional Works.

750.G15 ABANDONED VEHICLES

Any vehicle that is left on an arterial road or freeway for a period greater than 48 hours is deemed to be abandoned. With the approval of the Superintendent the Contractor may shift a vehicle which the Contractor has reasonable cause to believe has been abandoned and is a hazard to road users. The Contractor shall take care not to damage vehicles when moving them and arrange for Victoria Police to access locked vehicles.

For any vehicle which the Contractor suspects has been abandoned, the time, date, location and registration details shall be recorded and the Superintendent advised within 24 hours of the initial sighting of the vehicle. If the Superintendent advises that the vehicle is stolen, the Contractor shall have no further involvement. If the vehicle is not stolen, the Contractor shall affix an 'abandoned vehicle sticker' (provided by the Superintendent) to the vehicle 48 hours after the time of initial sighting and advise the Superintendent. The Superintendent is responsible for making arrangements for the removal of an abandoned vehicle.

750.G16 PRIVATE ROAD STRUCTURES

Persons wanting to erect structures on road reserves must obtain the consent in writing of the Superintendent. Any new letter boxes, signs or other structures erected in the road reserve shall be reported to the Superintendent.

750.G17 PRIVATE ENTRANCES

Any person wishing to construct a private entrance, or widen an existing private entrance, to a property, must first obtain consent in writing of the Superintendent.

Maintenance of the surfacing of a private entrance beyond the outer edge of the shoulder is the responsibility of the owner.

Maintenance of drainage through all private entrance culverts shall be undertaken as Provisional Works. The Superintendent will, in consultation with the Contractor, nominate and approve the private entrance culverts to be maintained by the Contractor, or be done by others.

Where road openings have been done by others, the Superintendent may direct that maintenance of any temporary repair or final restoration be carried out by the Contractor as Provisional Works.
