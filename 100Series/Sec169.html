SECTION 169 - BRIDGEWORK CARRIED OUT OVER, ON OR ADJACENT TO RAILWAY PROPERTY

169.01 GENERAL

This section covers the requirements for the construction of bridgeworks over, on or adjacent to railway property.

169.02 APPROVAL TO PROCEED

All work carried out on railway property shall be subject to the requirements of the Railway Maintenance Manager or other appropriate officer of the railway authority and it will be the responsibility of the Superintendent to liaise with this officer.

HP No work shall be commenced over, on or adjacent to railway property without the permission of the Superintendent being obtained, and any such work shall continue only to the extent indicated by the Superintendent.

169.03 ACCESS

The Superintendent will arrange with the railway officer for adequate access to those portions of the railway property required so that the work may proceed without undue delay or hindrance.

Access will be arranged in such areas and at such times as nearly as possible as the programme of works necessitates. In the event of any delay to the Contractor's operations being caused by access to the railway property not being available when needed, the Contractor will be entitled to claim an extension of time for completion beyond the specified dates of practical completion for the whole of the works or separable works, whichever is relevant.

Such claims will be assessed by the Superintendent in accordance with the General Conditions of Contract.

169.04 RAILWAY CLEARANCES

In any work adjacent to existing rail tracks, the Contractor shall ensure that at all times formwork, staging, scaffolding, struts or stays shall conform to the limitations of the railway clearance diagram.

169.05 INTERFERENCE AND LIABILITY

All works on or adjacent to the railway property shall be carried out by the Contractor in such a manner as not to cause, or to be the cause of, interference or damage to any property or rolling stock or anything whatsoever belonging to the railway authority, and in such a manner as not to interfere in any way with the traffic or business of the railways or with any other work or contract that may be in progress on any railway line or premises.

HP No railway premises or property shall on any account or for any reason or cause whatsoever be obstructed by the Contractor without the written permission of the Superintendent.

The Contractor shall:

(a) comply with all the by-laws, rules and regulations of the railway authority relevant to this contract and for the time being in force;

(b) at his own cost provide all necessary watchmen (excluding the cost of railway authority flagmen), shoring, lights, barriers, fences and gangways, and properly maintain the same; and

(c) take all other precautions which may be requisite for the convenience and safety of the public and the protection of life and property, or which may be required by the Superintendent in conjunction with the Railway Maintenance Manager or other appropriate officer.

Railway authority officers may require that a railway flagman be employed at the site to ensure that the requirements of the specification are satisfied. The cost of the flagman will be borne by VicRoads, but liquidated damages for the contract have been assessed taking this cost into account.


Rubber-tyred machinery, other than motor vehicles, shall be required to stop before crossing the railway tracks, and only proceed if no trains are in sight, unless signalled to proceed by a railway authority flagman.

All necessary precautions shall be taken to ensure that high loads do not foul overhead wires or cables when crossing tracks.

Construction plant, such as cranes, excavators etc., shall be made stationary and aerial elements (booms, buckets etc.) grounded during the passage of a train.

The Contractor shall notify the Superintendent of any damage caused to the property of the railway authority during the progress of the work as a result of the Contractor's operations. If so directed by the Superintendent, the Contractor shall restore such property at his own expense to the condition which, in the opinion of the Superintendent, existed prior to the commencement of the Contractor's operations.

In the case of any work or any property of the railway authority being damaged, it may be repaired by the railway authority and the cost of such repairs will be deducted by VicRoads from any moneys due, or which may from time to time become due, to the Contractor.

The Contractor shall be liable for the cost of any accident or any loss or damage whatsoever and to whomsoever which may occur or be occasioned on the premises of the railway authority through or owing to or in any way arising out of his neglect or misconduct or that of any of the persons in his employment or under his control.

In the event of the Contractor's failure or neglect to carry out the works and provide, maintain and take all precautions in strict conformity with, and with due observance of the provisions herein contained, then, in every such case as often as the same shall occur, the Contractor will be held responsible and liable for any loss or damage whatsoever which in the judgement of the Superintendent shall have been sustained by the railway authority owing to any such failure or neglect by or on the part of the Contractor and the amount of any such loss or damage as assessed by the Superintendent may be deducted by VicRoads from any money due or which may become due to the Contractor.

169.06 CONSTRUCTION OF SPANS OVER RAILWAY LINES

During the erection of spans over railway lines, the Contractor shall be responsible for taking all possible measures to ensure the safety of the workmen against risk of falling onto the tracks or being struck by trains, and likewise to protect the travelling public and the property of the railway authority from damage caused by materials falling or projecting from the new structure, or the staging used in its erection.

For this purpose the following precautions shall be taken:

(a) Traffic across the railway tracks at the site shall be kept to a minimum, and limited to a selected point where the visibility is unrestricted both up and down the tracks for a person at least 3 m back from either side of the track.

Where it is necessary for material to be moved across the tracks the movement shall take place at times when the Contractor has confirmed that the tracks will be clear of rail traffic for a reasonable period.

It will be the responsibility of the Contractor to ascertain the movement of trains on any particular day, and to regulate his operations accordingly.

(b) The times at which superstructure components are placed over the rail track shall be subject to review by the Superintendent.

(c) All parts of the overhead system, including conductors, contact wires, catenary wires, dropper arms, pull off arms, and any other overhead fittings, whether in service or newly erected, shall always be considered alive at 1500 volts and treated accordingly, unless definite instructions have been given in writing by the railway authority that such conductors wires and the like, are dead and earthed.

Where workmen are carrying out work of any kind over or adjacent to the railway tracks, care must be exercised to avoid touching any part of the overhead system with any part of their body or clothing, or with any article which they may be carrying.


Metal tapes or metal reinforced tapes shall not be used in positions where they are likely to fall or be blown across any overhead conductors, wires, and any other overhead fittings, unless a "Permit to Work" has been obtained from the railway authority. Attention is also drawn to the danger of throwing or allowing paint, water or any other liquid, or materials such as rope, wire, etc., with which workmen or parts of the structure may be in contact, to fall on the overhead equipment.

HP No work shall be carried out on any portion of the structure over or adjacent to the railway tracks and in the proximity of the overhead electrical equipment except in the presence of a representative of the railway authority who is certified as qualified to distinguish between safe and unsafe conditions, and until a "Permit to Work" has been obtained.

The times shown on the "Permit to Work" shall be strictly adhered to, and the permit shall be returned by the Contractor to the railway authority not later than the time shown, whether the work is completed or not. The permit will then be cancelled and will be of no further value.

The Contractor shall apply to the Superintendent for the issue of a new permit for each and every period during which work will be carried out in places likely to be in dangerous proximity to the overhead electrical equipment.

The cost of attendance of the necessary railway staff to provide protection, earth overhead wiring etc., will be borne by VicRoads.

After the main beams or slabs have been erected, protective shields will be fitted over the overhead wiring by the railway authority without charge to the Contractor.

Work may then be permitted above the overhead wires in the "live" condition subject to the review by the Superintendent.

In addition to the above, the Contractor shall take such additional precautions as may be required from time to time by the Superintendent.

169.07 RAILWAY SIGNALLING AND COMMUNICATION FACILITIES

The Contractor shall note that railway signalling and communication facilities exist on and adjacent to the site of the works, and shall carry out the work to avoid any interference with this equipment.

The Superintendent will arrange for any alterations required to such facilities during the contract without cost to the Contractor.

The Contractor shall keep the rails free of any form of contact with metallic objects, as all tracks in the area control electric circuits of the signalling system. Metal tapes or metal reinforced CED tapes shall not be used across running tracks unless a piece of approved insulating material is placed between the tape and rails.

Where a pathway or roadway is permitted over the rails, a ballast or bitumen roadway or timber panels shall be provided by the Contractor, and a thick coating of emulsified bitumen shall be applied to the sides and bottoms of the rails.

The contractor shall use rubber tyred vehicles wherever possible to prevent fouling of the tracks and shorting of the track circuits.

Railway tracks shall not be crossed with steel wheeled or tracked construction plant without the permission of the Superintendent.

Rail bonding and track circuit connections shall not be disturbed by the Contractor, and the railway authority shall be informed immediately if these connections are affected.

The Contractor shall not in any way cause any interference with the normal operation of any points or signal equipment in the works area. The points, signal wires and point rodding shall be kept free of any material or equipment.

The Contractor shall carry out all works over or adjacent to the railway tracks in such a manner as not to cause interference to the view of any railway fixed signals of any train driver or crew.

Any temporary lighting to protect the works shall be provided and positioned to avoid confusion with signals. White lights only shall be used for temporary lighting and placement shall be acceptable to the Superintendent.
